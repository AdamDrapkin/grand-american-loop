## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY — Top 12 Findings Across Both Parks

1. **Your Glacier dates (Jun 22–25) are the single best window of the summer.** The new 2026 Logan Pass shuttle system and 3-hour parking limit don't launch until July 1 — you drive freely, park without time limit, and face lighter crowds than July. This is not a small advantage.

2. **Canyon Village is the best possible base in Yellowstone.** Every major site (Grand Canyon rims, Hayden Valley, Grand Prismatic, Old Faithful, Lamar Valley) is within 45 minutes. No other campground comes close for this combination.

3. **Arrive at every major attraction before 9am OR after 4pm.** This is the single most-cited piece of advice across all 1,635 social media items and every article reviewed. There are no exceptions. Midday equals full parking, long lines, and degraded experience.

4. **Dunraven Pass (Canyon–Tower/Lamar) has major reconstruction in 2026.** Call 307-344-2117 the morning of any planned Lamar Valley trip. If closed, the detour via Norris adds 45 minutes each way.

5. **Mountain goats at Logan Pass are as close to guaranteed as wildlife gets.** Park sources and social media unanimously confirm this — described as "about as close to a guarantee as wildlife viewing gets." Your June 22–25 visit is peak goat season.

6. **Grand Prismatic Spring from the Fairy Falls Overlook trail (1.2 mi RT) is objectively better than the boardwalk.** The boardwalk puts you right next to it but you can't see the colors — the overlook trail gives the iconic aerial rainbow view. Use the Fairy Falls trailhead parking (not the main Midway lot, which fills by 10am).

7. **Hayden Valley is 5 miles from your tent and delivers grizzly bear sightings from the car.** Every morning and evening driving to/from Grand Prismatic or Old Faithful, you pass through it. Zero extra effort. This is Yellowstone's answer to Lamar Valley with a fraction of the driving.

8. **Artist Point (0.3 mi paved) is the best 15-minute investment in Yellowstone.** The most photographed viewpoint in the park. Completely flat, fully paved, wheelchair accessible. Do it before 9am for South Rim morning light on the falls.

9. **Hidden Lake Overlook at Glacier will likely have snow in late June.** The trail starts at 6,646 ft. The boardwalk can be buried. Microspikes and waterproof boots strongly recommended. Still very doable — yellow road markers guide you when the path is invisible.

10. **Going-to-the-Sun Road is "the drive of a lifetime."** @whatrvdoing's reel has 2.2 million views and it's just someone driving the road. @ourfulltimeadventure posted two separate Glacier reels with 15.8M and 11.7M views. The road alone is the attraction — driving it slowly with pullouts is not secondary to the trails.

11. **Wild Goose Island Overlook is 5–10 minutes from your St. Mary campsite and might be the single best photo in Glacier.** Tiny island in St. Mary Lake backed by 8,000-ft peaks. This is the opening sequence of *The Shining*. Best at sunrise — just drive west on GTTS Road before dawn and pull off.

12. **Bison are everywhere and all traffic stops.** TikTok's top 2 Yellowstone videos (29M + 24M views) are bison walking down a road. Budget 20+ minutes extra on any Hayden Valley or Lamar Valley drive. Stay in the vehicle, don't honk, they move on their own.

#### 1. GRAND CANYON OF THE YELLOWSTONE ★★★★★
*Right outside your campsite. Do both rims.*

**Artist Point** (South Rim) — **START HERE**
- Distance: 0.3 miles RT · Elevation: 22 ft · Surface: fully paved, wheelchair-accessible
- The most photographed viewpoint in Yellowstone. Head-on view of 308-ft Lower Falls framed by yellow/orange canyon walls. Rainbow in mist on sunny mornings.
- **Best time:** South Rim gets morning light. Arrive **before 9am**.
- Parking: Largest Canyon lot — still fills fast. Arrive before 9am or use Lower Falls lot and walk in.
- Walk ~1/8 mile past the main overlook for views most visitors never find.
- **TikTok verdict:** Artist Point appears in the top search results for nearly every "Yellowstone tips" query. Universal recommendation.

**Brink of Lower Falls** (North Rim) — **DO THIS TOO**
- Distance: 0.8 miles RT · Elevation drop: 600 ft (10 paved switchbacks) · Surface: fully paved
- Puts you at the lip of the 308-ft falls — mist, roar, one of the best views in the park.
- NOT accessible — steep paved switchbacks with steps. Not suitable for heart/lung conditions or fear of heights. 31-min average time.
- **Best time:** North Rim gets afternoon light. Do after Artist Point.
- Parking: North Rim Drive is one-way — once you enter, you cannot turn back. Plan accordingly.

**Inspiration Point** (North Rim)
- Short walk from pullout. Recently renovated accessible overlooks. No falls view, but dramatic downstream canyon stretching to the horizon. Best at golden hour.

**South Rim Trail** (Chittenden Bridge → Artist Point)
- 2 miles one-way, mostly flat along the rim. Connect viewpoints on foot instead of driving.

**Uncle Tom's Trail** (South Rim) — Optional for the brave
- 328 open-grate metal steps bolted into the canyon wall. Very short. Best close-up of Lower Falls. Not for anyone with height issues.

**How long for both rims:** 3–4 hours including parking and walks.  
**Best strategy:** South Rim (Artist Point) in the morning, North Rim (Brink + Grand View + Inspiration) in the afternoon.

#### 2. HAYDEN VALLEY ★★★★★
*5 miles south of Canyon Junction. You drive past it every time you head to Old Faithful or Grand Prismatic.*

- **No walking required.** All wildlife viewing from vehicle pullouts.
- June is prime: grizzlies foraging, bison with calves, wolves, elk, coyotes, bald eagles, ospreys, trumpeter swans, sandhill cranes, river otters.
- **Dawn window: 5:30–8:00am.** Best probability for grizzly bears.
- **Secondary morning: 8–11am.** Still productive.
- **Evening: 5–8pm.** Second best window.
- Grizzly Overlook at the north end: short walk across road and up a low hill for 360° valley views.
- **PLAN:** Every morning leaving Canyon Village, and every evening returning, slow down through Hayden Valley and scan the slopes with binoculars. It costs you 15 minutes and delivers some of the best wildlife you'll see in North America.

---

#### 3. GRAND PRISMATIC SPRING / MIDWAY GEYSER BASIN ★★★★★
*~45 min southwest from Canyon Village (via Grand Loop toward Old Faithful)*

**USE THE FAIRY FALLS TRAILHEAD — NOT THE MAIN MIDWAY LOT**
- Main Midway lot fills by 9–10am, no overflow. Fairy Falls has two lots and is easier.
- From Fairy Falls lot: walk 1.2 miles RT (gradual uphill) to **Grand Prismatic Overlook** — the aerial rainbow view everyone has seen on TikTok.
- From Midway lot: walk the 1.0-mile boardwalk loop (flat, paved, accessible) — gets you close but can't see the colors from ground level.
- **Best photo strategy:** Arrive early via Fairy Falls → overlook first (colors build as sun rises), then boardwalk.
- **Best color viewing time:** Midday light brings out the blues and oranges most vividly. Early morning has more steam (dramatic but obscures colors). Trade-off: early for solitude, midday for colors, **7–9am on a clear day for the sweet spot.**
- Colors visible: brilliant blue center (240°F water), orange/red/yellow rings (thermophilic bacteria living in cooling water zones).
- No permit required for either the overlook trail or the boardwalk.

**TikTok insight:** @shondamerrick's video "Here's what NOT to do at Yellowstone — Do not go off the boardwalk" has 6.6M views. The boardwalk boundary is real and enforced. Stay on it.

#### 4. OLD FAITHFUL ★★★★☆
*~35–45 min southwest from Canyon Village (past Hayden Valley, Grand Prismatic)*

- Erupts every **~90 minutes**. Check predicted times on the board at the Visitor Center.
- Average eruption lasts 1–5 minutes.
- **Parking:** Fills by 10–10:30am. Arrive before 11am or after 5pm. **Do not leave immediately after an eruption** — you'll be in the same traffic wave as everyone else. Walk the Upper Geyser Basin loop for 20–30 minutes instead.
- **Three ways to watch:**
  1. Main boardwalk viewing area — the classic experience (free, paved, easy)
  2. Old Faithful Inn balcony — second-floor outdoor balcony above the crowd (no entry fee needed for balcony)
  3. Observation Point Trail (1.6 mi RT) — elevated bird's-eye view, less crowded, truly spectacular

**Upper Geyser Basin Loop**  
- 2-hour walk on boardwalk trails past Castle Geyser, Grand Geyser, Riverside Geyser, and Morning Glory Pool.
- Mostly flat, paved/boardwalk. No permit. The basin has more active geysers per square mile than anywhere else on Earth.
- Combine with Old Faithful for a 2–4 hour visit.

**PLAN FOR OLD FAITHFUL DAY:** Leave Canyon by 7am → Hayden Valley drive → Midway (Grand Prismatic overlook 9am) → Midway boardwalk → Old Faithful (~11am, check eruption schedule) → Upper Geyser Basin walk → drive back through Hayden Valley at 5pm.

#### 5. LAMAR VALLEY ★★★★★ (Dawn Trips Only)
*~1–1.5 hrs northeast from Canyon Village. CHECK DUNRAVEN PASS ROAD STATUS FIRST.*

- **Call 307-344-2117 morning of June 27** before planning this drive. If Dunraven Pass is closed for construction, route via Norris adds 45 minutes each way.
- **Leave Canyon Village by 4:30–5:00am** for dawn wildlife viewing. Sunrise is ~5:30am in late June.
- Best wildlife window: **5:30–8:00am.** Then again 5–8pm.
- All viewing is from vehicle and roadside pullouts. No hiking required.
- Look for cars pulled over with spotting scopes — that tells you there's something to see. Stop safely at the next pullout.
- **Wolves:** Look for the Junction Butte and Lamar Canyon packs. Best sightings near Slough Creek pullout and Lamar River Bridge. Bring 10x+ binoculars or a spotting scope.
- **Bison:** Guaranteed. Bison jams here can last 20+ minutes.
- **Grizzlies:** Calving season brings bears to the meadows — June is prime.
- Wolf sighting: realistic but not guaranteed. Bison and grizzly: very likely.

**Trout Lake Trail** (near Lamar Valley)  
- 1.2 miles RT, easy, crystal-clear lake, river otters possible
- No permit needed — a great add-on if you've been Lamar Valley wildlife watching

#### 1. WILD GOOSE ISLAND OVERLOOK ★★★★★ (Free, 0 mi hike, 5 min from campsite)
*Your single best sunrise shot in the entire park*

- **Location:** 6.7 miles west of St. Mary Visitor Center on GTTS Road — **5–10 minute drive from St. Mary.**
- **What you see:** A 14-foot island in St. Mary Lake backed by 8,000-ft peaks (Red Eagle, Little Chief, Dusty Star, Citadel). This is the opening sequence of *The Shining*. Instantly recognizable.
- **Best time: Sunrise.** Faces southwest — peaks catch first light at dawn. Arrive 30 minutes before sunrise.
- Long paved parking pullout on the lake side of road. No hiking needed.
- **@earthpix's Glacier reel showing "the famous colorful rocks" has 3.2M Instagram views.** The lake scenery is consistently one of the top-performing Glacier topics on social.
- This requires zero effort and could be your best photo of the entire 18-day trip.

#### 2. GOING-TO-THE-SUN ROAD DRIVE ★★★★★ (Vehicle, No Walking Required)
*@whatrvdoing: "You neeeed to drive here once in your life!" — 2.2M TikTok views. Just from driving the road.*

Drive the entire GTTS Road east-to-west at least once, slowly, with multiple pullout stops:

**East Side (from St. Mary heading west):**
- St. Mary Visitor Center → Two Dog Flats (scan for bears from road) → Rising Sun → Sun Point Pullout → Sunrift Gorge → St. Mary Falls Trailhead → continue west

**West Side (past Logan Pass heading east is also worth doing for a different perspective):**

#### 3. LOGAN PASS ★★★★★
*Mountain goats near-guaranteed. Best alpine meadow in the park.*

**Getting There (June 22–25):**
- Drive directly — no shuttle required (shuttle season starts July 1)
- No parking time limit (enforcement starts July 1)
- Parking fills by **7:00–7:10am** on peak days. **Arrive by 6–6:30am for morning, OR after 3:30–4:30pm** for late afternoon (families leave for dinner, spots open up)
- If you miss parking: wait 10–15 minutes at the edge — turnover happens regularly

**Mountain Goats:**
- Described universally as "as close to guaranteed as wildlife viewing gets"
- Goats with kids visible in the open meadow starting from the parking lot
- Do NOT need to hike — goats graze right at the visitor center area
- June is peak season: post-snow melt, high-nutrition vegetation, perfect goat visibility

**Hidden Lake Overlook** ✅ (within filter: 2.7 mi RT)
- Distance: 2.7 miles RT · Elevation: 551 ft
- **Likely snow in late June** — boardwalk may be buried, trail partially invisible
- Yellow road markers guide you through snowpack. Microspikes recommended.
- Even with snow it's very doable and beautiful — the snow IS part of the experience
- If trail is closed due to bear activity, Logan Pass area still worth the drive for goats alone
- Time: 1–1.5 hours with stops

**What to bring to Logan Pass:** Warm layers, windproof jacket, waterproof boots, microspikes, bear spray, sunscreen (UV is intense at elevation).

#### 4. ST. MARY FALLS + VIRGINIA FALLS ★★★★☆
*Best short hike from your east side base. Two waterfalls.*

- Distance: ~3.2 miles RT (from shuttle stop) or ~3.6 miles RT from trailhead
- Elevation: Descend 260 ft to St. Mary Falls, climb 285 ft to Virginia Falls
- **St. Mary Falls** (0.8 mi one-way from shuttle stop): 35-ft waterfall, three tiers, brilliant aqua-green pools over red rock. One of the most photogenic hikes in the park.
- **Virginia Falls** (1.6 mi one-way from shuttle stop): Larger, more dramatic. The extra half-mile is worth it.
- No permit required. No paid entry beyond park fee.
- Parking: St. Mary Falls Shuttle Stop parking area; or park at the trailhead
- Best morning hike when you don't want to drive to Logan Pass or Many Glacier.
- Time: 2–2.5 hours for both falls combined.

#### 5. SUN POINT NATURE TRAIL ★★★★☆
*Best easy lake view near St. Mary*

- Distance: 1.6 miles RT (0.8 mi one-way to Sun Point)
- Rocky peninsula, 100 ft above St. Mary Lake, panoramic mountain views
- Descends 250 ft (returns same), easy terrain
- Also connects to Baring Falls (0.3 mi one-way, 25-ft waterfall) and Sunrift Gorge (5-minute walk)
- Ideal for a mellow morning or evening when you want a lake view without the GTTS Road drive
- Time: 45–60 minutes

#### 6. MANY GLACIER VALLEY ★★★★★
*Confirm 2026 access at St. Mary VC before planning this day*

**Getting there from St. Mary:**
- Drive north on US-89 ~12 miles, turn left at Babb, ~12 miles west into the valley
- **~35–40 minutes from St. Mary**
- Check Many Glacier 2026 access status — required shuttle tickets in 2025 (water system construction). If shuttle still required, get tickets at 7 PM MDT the night before on Recreation.gov.
- Arrive **before 7–8am** for parking, or after 5pm.

**Ranger Wildlife Scopes:**
- Park rangers often stationed at the Swiftcurrent Motor Lodge parking lot with spotting scopes aimed at "mountain dandruff" (goats high on cliff faces). Just pull in and look for the scope.

**Short Walks (all pass the filter):**

**Swiftcurrent Nature Trail (Lake Loop)** ✅ (2.3–2.6 mi RT)
- Loops around Swiftcurrent Lake with Many Glacier Hotel in the frame, footbridge over the creek
- First 0.9 miles from hotel is wheelchair-accessible
- Watch for moose in the wetlands, goats on the cliffs above, bears in the forested section
- Time: 1–1.5 hours

**Redrock Falls** ✅ (3.6 mi RT, nearly FLAT)
- Flattest hike in Many Glacier — only 100 ft elevation gain
- Along Swiftcurrent Lake and Lake Josephine shores to vivid red argillite rock waterfall
- Best family option for distance-to-reward in the valley
- Time: 2–2.5 hours

**Apikuni Falls** ✅ (2 mi RT, steeper)
- 700 ft elevation gain over 1 mile — a real workout but short
- Starts 1.1 miles east of Many Glacier Hotel
- Time: 1.5–2 hours
