## Routing Architecture Notes

### Redwood — RESOLVED
- Redwood National Park (Crescent City) = 6 hrs 16 mins / 356 mi NORTH of SF → breaks southbound route
- **Solution**: Muir Woods National Monument (45 min from SF) = same ancient coast redwoods, zero detour
- User confirmed: redwood *experience* is the goal, not the specific park name
- Muir Woods is the answer. Book in advance (timed entry required).

### Inland Loop (Tahoe → Yosemite → Sequoia)
- This cluster adds ~3.5 days of driving to the trip inland and back
- Lake Tahoe can be Day 2-3 (east detour from SF)
- Yosemite to Sequoia is logical consecutive days
- Sequoia back to coast (Big Sur) = 4h 29m — longest single driving day
- This inland arc is the backbone challenge of the 10-day plan

### Coastal Arc (Big Sur → Santa Barbara → LA)
- After returning from Sequoia to the coast, this stretch runs beautifully
- Big Sur needs a full day — cannot be rushed
- Paso Robles sits naturally between Big Sur and Santa Barbara (wine country stop)
- Santa Barbara → Malibu → Santa Monica → Venice → LA is the final 2 days

## Flags & Watchpoints
- **Alcatraz**: Must book ferry tickets 2-4 weeks in advance. Sells out fast in summer.
- **Muir Woods**: Requires advance parking/shuttle reservations (recreation.gov). No walk-up parking allowed.
- **Yosemite**: Day-use reservations required Apr–Oct. Valley can be very crowded. Book lodging 6+ months out.
- **Sequoia**: Less reservation-dependent than Yosemite but Giant Forest can be busy in summer.
- **Big Sur**: Highway 1 closures possible after storms (check Caltrans before driving). Very limited lodging — book early.
- **Paso Robles**: Good overnight stop between Big Sur and Santa Barbara — wineries, farm-to-table dining.
