Jun 22HV, PA → Pittsburgh308 mi · 4h 52m▼

🚗 Drive Summary

I-276 connects Huntington Valley directly to the PA Turnpike (I-76). You'll cross five Appalachian ridges through tunnels — Kittatinny, Tuscarora, and Allegheny — before descending into Pittsburgh via I-376. America's first modern superhighway, opened 1940. Beautiful mountain views at Somerset and Bedford.

📍 About This Stop

Pittsburgh sits at the confluence of three rivers. A worthy first-night stop rather than just a fuel break. The optional 15-minute detour to the Duquesne Incline (Mount Washington) gives you the best city skyline view on the East Coast. Primanti Bros. is a Pittsburgh icon — get the sandwich with coleslaw and fries stuffed inside.

☀️ June Conditions

74–84°F, humid, lush green hills in full summer canopy. Sunset at 8:48pm EDT — long driving day. Afternoon thunderstorms possible (3–7pm) over the Alleghenies. Tunnels stay dry but check radar before the mountain crossing.

🚦 Traffic Advisory

Leave Huntington Valley by 7am to avoid I-276 Philadelphia suburb congestion. Pittsburgh I-376 backs up 4–6pm — time your arrival accordingly. PA Turnpike has tolls throughout; keep E-ZPass accessible or budget ~$25 cash.

🛌 Rest Stops (Sleep)

  • Sideling Hill Service Plaza (I-76, MM 172) — 24hr, designated truck lot, quiet overnight
  • Allegheny Mountain Service Plaza (I-76, MM 178) — elevated, cool at night
  • New Stanton Service Plaza (I-76, near Pittsburgh) — last plaza before city

🚿 Showers (Truck Stops)

  • Flying J Travel Center, New Stanton PA (I-76 Exit 75B / I-70 Exit 75) — 12 showers, $15
  • TA Travel Center, Carlisle PA (I-81 Exit 52, near I-76) — 22 showers, $15
  • Pilot Travel Center, Breezewood PA (I-70/I-76 junction) — major truck hub

🍳 Food Along the Way

  • Hoss's Family Steak & Sea, Somerset PA — classic mid-PA highway diner, open late
  • Eat'n Park, multiple Pittsburgh locations — local chain, open 24hr
  • Primanti Bros., Pittsburgh — iconic sandwich with coleslaw + fries inside the bread
  • Roy Rogers at most PA Turnpike service plazas

Jun 23Pittsburgh → Indianapolis359 mi · 5h 33m▼

🚗 Drive Summary

I-376 west clips through the West Virginia panhandle almost imperceptibly before joining I-70 in Ohio. Rolling farmland through Wheeling, then Columbus via the I-270 bypass, Dayton, crossing into Indiana at Richmond. Flat and efficient — the kind of driving that makes miles disappear.

📍 About This Stop

Indianapolis rewards a short stop. Monument Circle at the city center is walkable in 10 minutes. Mass Ave arts district has good restaurants. The Indy 500 track is right off I-465 if you want to see the most famous oval in racing up close.

☀️ June Conditions

80–90°F, humid. Ohio and Indiana get strong afternoon thunderstorm lines in June — sometimes severe with hail and gusty winds. Check radar at each fuel stop after noon. Corn and soy are chest-high by late June; the green is vivid.

🚦 Traffic Advisory

Columbus I-70/I-270 merge is the main choke point — worst 4–6pm, avoid if possible. Indianapolis I-465 beltway backs up midafternoon on weekdays. Aim to be past Columbus before 12pm.

🛌 Rest Stops (Sleep)

  • Ohio I-70 rest areas spaced ~50 miles: Clark County, Preble County, Guernsey County — all 24hr
  • Indiana I-70 rest area, Fountain County — quiet, overnight trucks welcome
  • Indiana State Fairgrounds RV area, Indianapolis — urban camping fallback

🚿 Showers (Truck Stops)

  • Love's Travel Stop, Richmond IN (I-70 Exit 145) — right on route, $15 showers, 24hr
  • Flying J, Greenfield IN (I-70 Exit 96, near Indianapolis KOA) — convenient combo stop
  • Pilot Travel Center, Columbus OH (I-70 West) — multiple locations, all with showers

🍳 Food Along the Way

  • White Castle, Dayton or Columbus area — Midwest original, minimum 1 visit required by law
  • Waffle House, multiple I-70 Ohio exits — 24hr, $8 breakfast
  • Skyline Chili, Dayton OH — Cincinnati-style chili on spaghetti, a genuine regional experience
  • Shapiro's Delicatessen, Indianapolis — legendary deli open since 1905

Jun 24Indianapolis → Kansas City482 mi · 7h 19m▼

🚗 Drive Summary

I-70 West is pure Midwest: corn and soybeans, flat as a griddle, enormous sky. Terre Haute, then into Illinois. The Mississippi River crossing into St. Louis (Poplar Street Bridge) is a genuine moment — you can see the Gateway Arch perfectly from the highway. Missouri hills roll west toward Kansas City.

📍 About This Stop

Kansas City straddles the MO/KS state line and is genuinely underrated. Its BBQ reputation is earned — Bryant's, Joe's KC, Jack Stack, and Gates are all legitimate institutions. The River Market neighborhood near downtown has nightlife and farmer's markets. You'll sleep well here.

☀️ June Conditions

84–92°F, very humid. June is peak tornado season across Missouri and Kansas. Storms can develop fast in the afternoon. Check weather apps before the 3pm window. The air smells like rain and corn.

🚦 Traffic Advisory

St. Louis I-70 through the downtown interchange ('the Curve') is one of America's worst traffic chokepoints — avoid 4–6pm weekdays. The I-270 north bypass around St. Louis saves 20+ min. Kansas City I-70/I-435 interchange can back up from 4–6pm.

🛌 Rest Stops (Sleep)

  • Missouri I-70 rest areas: Danville, Concordia, Blue Springs — all maintained, overnight parking fine
  • Illinois I-70 rest areas: Troy and Highland — clean, 24hr
  • St. Louis Arch grounds — public restrooms, parking (paid), good stretch break

🚿 Showers (Truck Stops)

  • Love's Travel Stop, Foristell MO (I-70 Exit 203) — between St. Louis and KC, $15
  • Pilot Flying J, Grain Valley MO (I-70, east of KC) — last stop before city
  • Pilot Travel Center, Terre Haute IN (I-70 Exit 7) — west end of Indiana, clean facilities

🍳 Food Along the Way

  • Gates Bar-B-Q, Kansas City — original KC BBQ, get the ribs and the 'hi may I help you' greeting
  • Joe's KC Bar-B-Que (formerly Oklahoma Joe's) — legendary brisket, inside a gas station
  • Ted Drewes Frozen Custard, St. Louis — summer institution (25 min detour off I-44 in St. Louis)
  • Imo's Pizza, St. Louis area — 'square beyond compare' provel cheese pizza

Jun 25Kansas City → Denver + Red Rocks603 mi · 8h 41m▼

🚗 Drive Summary

The longest day: 603 miles. I-70 west through Kansas is relentlessly flat for the first 200 miles — embrace the horizon and the enormous sky. By Salina (central KS) the Smoky Hills emerge; by Hays the landscape becomes dramatic chalk formations. Cross into Colorado at Kanorado. Denver rises against the entire Front Range. End at Red Rocks Amphitheatre (Morrison, CO) for sunset over the city.

📍 About This Stop

Red Rocks is a natural sandstone amphitheatre 15 miles west of Denver. The park is open even without a concert — hike the Trading Post Trail (1.4 miles, easy) for formation views and Denver panoramas. The red rock columns glow gold at sunset. This is your reward for the Kansas drive.

☀️ June Conditions

Kansas: 90–100°F, dry and powerful wind. Colorado Front Range: 80–88°F. Red Rocks evening: 65–72°F with dramatic evening thunderstorms building over the Rockies. Bring a layer for Red Rocks — temperatures drop 20°F after sunset. June lightning shows from high points are spectacular.

🚦 Traffic Advisory

I-70 Kansas is essentially empty. Denver I-70/I-25 interchange ('The Stack') is the worst in Colorado — avoid 4–6pm. Red Rocks access road (CO-470) backs up on summer evenings before concerts. Arrive Red Rocks before 5pm.

🛌 Rest Stops (Sleep)

  • Kansas I-70 rest areas: Colby, Russell, Salina — all 24hr, spacious truck lots
  • Colorado Welcome Center, Burlington CO (I-70 at CO border) — great rest stop
  • Eisenhower Tunnel parking area (I-70, not on this route but nearby reference) — scenic pull-off

🚿 Showers (Truck Stops)

  • Love's Travel Stop, Salina KS (I-70 Exit 252) — 12 showers, major facility
  • Pilot Travel Center, Colby KS (I-70 Exit 54) — 5 showers, mid-Kansas
  • Flying J, Limon CO (I-70 Exit 359) — 8 showers, last truck stop before Denver metro

🍳 Food Along the Way

  • Coors Brewery, Golden CO (I-70 Exit 265) — free tours, 15 min detour, genuinely worth it
  • Beau Jo's Pizza, Golden CO — Colorado-style mountain pie, great carb load
  • Gella's Diner, Hays KS — good mid-Kansas lunch stop in a fun craft brewery setting
  • Chef's Diner, Salina KS — classic 1950s roadside diner

Jun 26Denver → Rocky Mountain NP → Casper, WY~330 mi · 5h▼

🚗 Drive Summary

US-36 west from Denver through Boulder to Estes Park. Enter Rocky Mountain NP at the Fall River entrance. Trail Ridge Road — highest continuous paved highway in the US at 12,183 ft — crosses alpine tundra above the treeline with views over the entire Front Range. Exit via the west side, pick up US-34 to I-25 North, cruise 200 miles to Casper.

📍 About This Stop

Rocky Mountain NP has 300+ miles of trails and 150 lakes. Trail Ridge Road is the star: at the summit you walk on arctic tundra, spot pika and yellow-bellied marmots, and on a clear day see 100 miles of peaks. June is prime: wildflowers below 10,000 ft, elk in the valleys, and snow still capping the highest peaks for dramatic contrast.

☀️ June Conditions

⚠️ TIMED ENTRY PERMIT REQUIRED (May 22–Oct 12, 2026). Book at recreation.gov — only $2 + park pass ($35/vehicle). Sold out fast; book the day reservations open. Alpine summit: 40–55°F, strong wind. Afternoon thunderstorms build by 2pm — be off Trail Ridge Road by 1pm. Snow patches remain on north-facing slopes.

🚦 Traffic Advisory

US-36 Boulder to Estes Park backs up on summer weekends. Aim to be at the Fall River entrance gate before 9am. Estes Park town is congested all day in summer — fuel and food before 8am or after 4pm. Trail Ridge Road temporarily closes during lightning storms (20–45 min closures).

🛌 Rest Stops (Sleep)

  • Alpine Visitor Center, Trail Ridge Road (11,796 ft) — daytime break, stunning, restrooms
  • Fort Collins rest area, I-25 North — clean 24hr stop heading to Casper
  • Pilot Flying J, Casper WY (I-25/US-20) — overnight truck lot, safest sleep spot in Casper
  • Walmart Supercenter, Casper WY — free overnight parking, common stop

🚿 Showers (Truck Stops)

  • TA Travel Center, Fort Collins CO (I-25 Exit 268) — last full-service shower stop before the mountains
  • Pilot Flying J, Cheyenne WY (I-25, between Denver and Casper) — on-route stop
  • Pilot Flying J, Casper WY (I-25, US-20 junction) — large truck lot, 24hr, $15 showers

🍳 Food Along the Way

  • Smoky Mountain Bakery, Estes Park — breakfast before the park, best cinnamon rolls
  • The Notchtop Bakery & Café, Estes Park — RMNP-themed pastries and sandwiches
  • Santiagos Mexican Restaurant, Fort Collins (I-25 route) — fast, authentic, local institution
  • Dairy Queen, Casper WY — unironic Midwest classic reward for long day

Jun 27Casper → Yellowstone (Day 1)~185 mi · 3h▼

🚗 Drive Summary

Take US-20 west then US-16 west from Tensleep into Ten Sleep Canyon — the start of the Bighorn Scenic Byway. 58 miles of sculpted canyon walls, Shell Creek canyon, and Shell Falls (120-ft waterfall) pour through Bighorn National Forest. Emerge at Greybull WY, pick up US-14A to Cody. Enter Yellowstone via the East Entrance — the quietest gate in the park.

📍 About This Stop

Cody, WY was founded by Buffalo Bill Cody in 1896 and wears its Western identity proudly. The Buffalo Bill Center of the West is a world-class museum (5 museums in 1 building — give it 2 hours if you have it). The East Entrance route takes you through Wapiti Valley, considered one of the world's most scenic 50-mile drives by Teddy Roosevelt.

☀️ June Conditions

Cody: 88–96°F, dry. Bighorn mountains: 60–72°F, spectacular. Yellowstone: 55–70°F daytime, 35–42°F nights. Pack a 30°F sleeping bag for Yellowstone. Bears are highly active in June (cubs are new); carry bear spray. Bison calves visible throughout. Most roads open by June 1.

🚦 Traffic Advisory

No significant congestion until Yellowstone East Entrance gate, which can have 30–60 min waits on summer weekend mornings. Arrive the gate before 10am or after 4pm. Once inside, bison jams are inevitable (and awesome — just wait them out).

🛌 Rest Stops (Sleep)

  • Ten Sleep WY (US-16) — small town, public restrooms
  • Greybull WY — rest area with picnic tables, good lunch break
  • Cody WY visitor center — full restrooms, tourist info, free parking

🚿 Showers (Truck Stops)

  • Love's Travel Stop, Cody WY area — last full-service truck stop before the park
  • Yellowstone Campground shower houses — Bridge Bay and Canyon have coin-op showers ($3–$5) inside the park
  • Love's Travel Stop, Cody WY — last shower before entering the park, $15

🍳 Food Along the Way

  • Cassie's Supper Club, Cody WY — legendary steakhouse in a building with Wild West history, great for dinner
  • Beta Coffeehouse, Cody WY — excellent morning coffee before the scenic drive
  • Eatery at the Buffalo Bill Center gift shop — quick lunch in Cody
  • Old Faithful Inn Dining Room (if you arrive in time) — dinner inside a 1904 log lodge

Jun 28🌋 Yellowstone — Full Explore Day 20 mi▼

🚗 Drive Summary

Zero miles on the highway. Today is fully inside Yellowstone National Park. Classic loop: Old Faithful (catch the 7am eruption — smallest crowds) → Grand Prismatic Spring (most vivid colors before 9am) → Norris Geyser Basin (most acidic in the park) → afternoon drive to Lamar Valley for wolves, grizzlies, and bison herds → Grand Canyon of Yellowstone for sunset at Artist Point.

📍 About This Stop

Yellowstone sits atop a supervolcano — the largest in North America. The park has 10,000 geothermal features, more than any other place on Earth. In June: elk calving season, grizzlies foraging openly, bison with rust-colored calves. The Grand Canyon of Yellowstone has waterfalls twice the height of Niagara. This is one of the most singular places on the planet.

☀️ June Conditions

55–72°F daytime, 35–45°F nights — layers are essential, especially at Lamar Valley mornings. Afternoon thunderstorms build by 2pm and typically clear by 4pm. Wildflowers bloom at lower elevations. Mosquitoes emerge by late June — bring DEET. Carry bear spray everywhere — legally required in backcountry.

🚦 Traffic Advisory

Old Faithful parking lot fills by 9am — arrive early. Bison on the road are an experience, not an inconvenience (they have right of way; never honk). Lamar Valley is accessed via a narrow road — pull-offs fill up at sunrise/sunset. Grand Canyon of Yellowstone Artist Point gets crowded by 10am.

🛌 Rest Stops (Sleep)

  • All major visitor centers have restrooms: Old Faithful, Canyon, Mammoth, Fishing Bridge
  • Norris Geyser Basin — restrooms at trailhead
  • Lamar Valley pull-offs — no facilities, plan ahead

🚿 Showers (Truck Stops)

  • Bridge Bay Marina shower house — coin-operated ($3–$5), open June–Sept
  • Canyon campground showers — coin-operated, central location
  • Fishing Bridge RV Park showers — guests + day use

🍳 Food Along the Way

  • Old Faithful Inn Dining Room — dinner inside a 1904 hand-built log lodge; make reservations at xanterra.com
  • Lake Hotel Dining Room — more formal, views of Yellowstone Lake
  • Yellowstone General Stores: Canyon, Old Faithful, Bridge Bay — groceries, deli sandwiches
  • Picnic at Norris Geyser Basin or Nez Perce Creek pull-off — bring your own food

Jun 29Yellowstone → Salt Lake City322 mi · 4h 42m▼

🚗 Drive Summary

Exit Yellowstone via the South Entrance, immediately entering Grand Teton National Park. The Tetons are unlike any other mountain range — they rise directly from the valley floor with no foothills, straight up 7,000 feet. Stop at Jackson Lake Dam overlook (15 min, free) for the classic Teton panorama. Lunch in Jackson, WY, then US-89 south through the Snake River Plain to I-15 and Salt Lake City.

📍 About This Stop

Jackson, WY is touristy but earned. The iconic antler arch in Town Square is the photo. Grand Teton NP has no entry wait (Yellowstone crowds mostly don't make it this far in the morning). Salt Lake City surprises visitors with excellent food, the salt lake itself glowing pink with halophilic algae, and the Wasatch Range as backdrop.

☀️ June Conditions

Grand Teton: 65–78°F, spectacular visibility. Jackson, WY: 82–86°F. Snake River Valley: 88°F. Salt Lake City: 95–100°F in late June — it's a desert basin. Wasatch storms stay in the mountains. Great Salt Lake is accessible from I-80 via Antelope Island State Park.

🚦 Traffic Advisory

US-89 through Hoback Junction (south of Jackson) backs up on summer weekends — worst noon–4pm. Jackson town center is congested all day in July/August; in late June it's still manageable. Salt Lake City I-15 heavy during evening rush (5–6:30pm).

🛌 Rest Stops (Sleep)

  • Grand Teton NP rest areas — at Jackson Lake Junction, Colter Bay
  • Jackson, WY — multiple public restrooms in Town Square area
  • US-89 rest area, Bear Lake Valley UT — lovely scenic stop

🚿 Showers (Truck Stops)

  • Love's Travel Stop, Idaho Falls ID (if routing via US-26) — on the highway
  • Flying J, Tremonton UT (I-15 North of SLC) — last stop before the city
  • Pilot Flying J, Salt Lake City — multiple I-15 locations with showers

🍳 Food Along the Way

  • The Bunnery, Jackson WY — iconic breakfast, OSM (oat sunflower millet) pancakes, expect a wait but worth it
  • Million Dollar Cowboy Bar, Jackson WY — walk in, look at the saddle barstools, have one drink
  • Red Iguana, Salt Lake City — legendary Mexican mole, expect a wait
  • Crown Burger, Salt Lake City — local institution for burgers with pastrami

Jun 30SLC → Bonneville Salt Flats → Reno518 mi · 7h 22m▼

🚗 Drive Summary

I-80 west from Salt Lake City. In 90 minutes you're at Wendover — gateway to the Bonneville Salt Flats. Walk on the flats: 30,000 acres of pure white salt where hundreds of land speed records have been set. Back on I-80 across Nevada — 400 miles of high desert through Wells, Battle Mountain, Winnemucca, and the Humboldt Sink to Reno. Pure American West.

📍 About This Stop

Bonneville Salt Flats: arrive before 10am while temperatures are manageable. The reflections in the thin brine layer (present in June) make the horizon disappear. Drive onto the salt — it's BLM land and you're allowed to. Take photos in every direction. The Nevada stretch of I-80 is one of the loneliest highways in America — beautiful in its emptiness.

☀️ June Conditions

Utah desert: 95–105°F in the afternoon. Nevada I-80: 90–100°F. Start early — be on the salt by 8am. Constant headwinds on the Nevada high desert (15–25 mph). Bring 1 gallon of water per person minimum. No shade on the salt flats — hat and sunscreen required.

🚦 Traffic Advisory

I-80 in Utah and Nevada is rarely congested. Reno I-80 through downtown backs up near casinos and during shift changes at the large warehouses. The Reno arch on Virginia Street is the most photographed traffic jam.

🛌 Rest Stops (Sleep)

  • Bonneville Salt Flats access road — BLM free area, no facilities (pit toilet only)
  • Nevada I-80 rest area, Wells NV — spacious, 24hr, good for an overnight if needed
  • Nevada I-80 rest area, Lovelock NV — quiet, free overnight for vehicles

🚿 Showers (Truck Stops)

  • Pilot Flying J, Wendover UT/NV (I-80 Exit 410) — at the state line, $15, 24hr
  • Pilot Flying J, Battle Mountain NV (I-80 Exit 229) — mid-Nevada, clean
  • Love's Travel Stop, Winnemucca NV — 5 showers, well-maintained

🍳 Food Along the Way

  • Wendover Nugget Casino buffet (NV side) — reliable and cheap (<$15)
  • Louis' Basque Corner, Reno NV — legendary family-style Basque dinner, affordable and enormous portions
  • Star Broiler Casino, Battle Mountain NV — surprisingly good steaks
  • Awful Awful Burger, Reno NV — legendary Nevada burger, 1 lb patty

Jul 1Reno rest / Lake Tahoe preview0 mi▼

🚗 Drive Summary

Rest day — no highway driving required. Lake Tahoe is 35 minutes south of Reno via US-395 to SR-431 (Mt. Rose Highway). Drive the north shore (SR-28) through Incline Village to Kings Beach — a slow, beautiful road along the lake. Stop at Tahoe City for lunch. Push to Emerald Bay on the south shore (1 hr from Reno) for the most photographed view in the Sierra Nevada.

📍 About This Stop

Lake Tahoe at 6,225 ft elevation is the clearest large lake in North America — visibility to 70 feet. In June: alpine wildflowers blooming, snow still visible on peaks above 9,000 ft, water a luminous electric blue. Not yet overrun by July crowds. Kings Beach is shallow enough for a (cold) swim. Emerald Bay has a tiny island with a stone mansion — rent a kayak for $25/hr.

☀️ June Conditions

Lake Tahoe: 68–76°F, sunny and crisp. Water temperature: 58–65°F — cold but doable on a hot day. Reno itself: 90–95°F. You'll feel the altitude (6,200 ft) — altitude sickness is rare but pace yourself. Mountain afternoon thunderstorms build by 3pm over the peaks.

🚦 Traffic Advisory

Reno–Tahoe highway is clear on weekdays. Emerald Bay pull-off (Hwy 89 South Shore) gets very congested on summer weekends — arrive before 10am. Kings Beach fills by 11am on weekends. No traffic concerns for a mid-week visit.

🛌 Rest Stops (Sleep)

  • Pilot Flying J lot, Reno NV — sleep here (same stop as showers), 24hr, safe
  • Walmart Supercenter, Reno NV (S Virginia St) — free overnight vehicle parking
  • Love's Travel Stop, Fernley NV — quieter than Reno, good backup if Reno lots full

🚿 Showers (Truck Stops)

  • Reno KOA or your campground shower house — morning start
  • Tahoe State Recreation Area, Tahoe City — shower facilities available to day visitors ($5)
  • Sand Harbor State Park, Incline Village NV — beach showers

🍳 Food Along the Way

  • Squeeze In, Reno NV — famous omelets (150+ varieties), long waits on weekends, worth it
  • Tahoe House Bakery, Tahoe City CA — excellent coffee and pastries for the lake drive
  • Gar Woods Grill, Carnelian Bay CA — deck dining on the lake, spectacular mountain view
  • Sunnyside Restaurant, Tahoe City CA — Sunday brunch on the water

Jul 2Reno → San Francisco ✅ by 11am218 mi · 3h 35m▼

🚗 Drive Summary

Final leg: 218 miles, 3.5 hours. I-80 west to Truckee, then climb to Donner Pass (7,056 ft) — where the infamous Donner Party was stranded in 1846–47. The Donner Memorial State Park museum is right off I-80 (20 min, $8/person, genuinely fascinating). Descend into Auburn and Sacramento, cross the Central Valley at speed, climb Altamont Pass, and the Bay Area spreads out before you. Arrive via the Bay Bridge into San Francisco.

📍 About This Stop

3,161 miles from Huntington Valley. You made it. The Bay Bridge at sunrise in the fog is one of the great entrances to a great city. San Francisco greets you with sea air, Victorian houses, the smell of sourdough, and hills that will make your car hesitate. June Gloom (marine layer fog) is a feature, not a bug — it burns off by noon.

☀️ June Conditions

Sierra Nevada: 55–65°F, clear and clean. Sacramento Valley: 95–105°F, short 60-mile stretch. Bay Area: June Gloom — marine fog layer in morning (55–62°F), clearing to 65°F by afternoon. Fog is thick on the Bay Bridge at 7am. Bring a layer — SF is genuinely cold in June evenings.

🚦 Traffic Advisory

Leave Reno by 6:30–7:00am to arrive SF by 10:30–11am. I-80 Sacramento backs up from 7:30am on weekdays. Bay Bridge westbound toll ($7 FasTrak, $7 cash) — FasTrak lanes move faster. US-101 and I-80 through SF both congested after 8am.

🛌 Rest Stops (Sleep)

  • Donner Memorial State Park, Truckee CA — restrooms, museum, beautiful lakeside picnic
  • Weigh station rest area, Colfax CA — public restrooms, views of the American River Canyon
  • Sacramento Welcome Center, off I-80 — last major stop before Bay Area

🚿 Showers (Truck Stops)

  • Pilot Flying J, Sacramento CA (I-80) — last truck stop before SF, $15
  • TA Travel Center, West Sacramento CA — full facilities, 24hr
  • Lovelock NV rest area (previous night) — use this if you need a morning shower before departing Reno

🍳 Food Along the Way

  • Squeeze In, Truckee CA — sister location, last mountain breakfast before the descent
  • Ikeda's Country Market, Auburn CA (I-80 Exit 119) — legendary fruit stand, pies, and burgers since 1946
  • In-N-Out Burger, Sacramento — your first California burger. Animal style. Non-negotiable.
  • Upon arriving in SF: Mission burrito at La Taqueria or El Farolito — the real destination
