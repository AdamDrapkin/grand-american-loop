Hidden Lake Overlook
2.7 mi RT Moderate 551 ft gain 1.5–2 hrs Starts at 6,646 ft
📍 Open in Google Maps AllTrails ↗

The boardwalk from Logan Pass VC to the Hidden Lake Overlook is likely buried under snow in late June. Yellow road markers guide you through. The trail climbs through alpine meadow with goats on surrounding ridges and views extending to the Continental Divide.

Microspikes are not optional. The snow makes it more beautiful, not harder. Overlook is the destination — you don't need to descend to the lake itself.

GTTS Road West Side
  • Continue west past Logan Pass. Drive to Avalanche Creek and back.
  • Weeping Wall — a waterfall that flows over the road during snowmelt. Stop and stand under it.
    📍 Google Maps
  • Triple Arches — remarkable stone bridge construction visible from road
  • Avalanche Creek — end of restricted vehicle zone. Walk to the creek, then turn around.
  • Drive back east — completely different light and perspective on the return.
Evening — Return to St. Mary
  • Sunrift Gorge on the way back — 5-minute walk into a narrow rock gorge with water churning through it.
    📍 Google Maps
  • Two Dog Flats / Rising Sun — evening grizzly and elk scan. Slow way down.
  • Wild Goose Island — evening light on the lake as the sun drops. Go both morning and evening.

Morning Hike — Redrock Falls (Recommended)
Redrock Falls Trail
3.6 mi RT Easy ~100 ft gain 2–2.5 hrs Flattest in Many Glacier
📍 Open in Google Maps AllTrails ↗

The flattest significant hike in Many Glacier — only ~100 ft of elevation gain. Follows Swiftcurrent Lake and Lake Josephine to a vivid red argillite rock waterfall. The red color is iron-rich sediment from 1.5 billion years ago.

Best family option here. Easy pace, beautiful lake views, moose in the wetlands, goats visible on cliffs above.

Make noise on this trail — forested sections are prime bear habitat. Bear spray accessible, not buried in your pack.

— or if you prefer a shorter loop —

Swiftcurrent Lake Nature Trail
2.6 mi RT Easy ~30 ft gain 1–1.5 hrs First 0.9 mi accessible
📍 Open in Google Maps AllTrails ↗

Loops around Swiftcurrent Lake with Many Glacier Hotel in the backdrop. First 0.9 miles is wheelchair accessible. The wetlands section is prime moose habitat. Goats on cliffs above throughout.

Afternoon — Return South via St. Mary Falls
  • Leave Many Glacier by noon–12:30pm. Drive south back (35–40 min).
  • Stop at St. Mary Falls + Virginia Falls — right off GTTS Road near St. Mary.
St. Mary Falls + Virginia Falls
3.2 mi RT (both) Moderate Descend 260 ft · Climb 285 ft 2–2.5 hrs
📍 Open in Google Maps AllTrails ↗

St. Mary Falls (0.8 mi one-way): 35-ft waterfall dropping into brilliant aqua-green pools over red argillite rock. June runoff makes it even more vivid.

Virginia Falls (1.6 mi one-way): Larger and more dramatic. Do not turn back at St. Mary Falls — the extra half-mile is always worth it.

Park at the St. Mary Falls Trailhead on GTTS Road. Late afternoon (2–4pm) is a sweet window — crowds thin and the light in the canyon is warm.
