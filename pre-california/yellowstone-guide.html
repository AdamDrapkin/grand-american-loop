June 26 · Full Day 1
Go South — The Geothermal Loop
Hayden Valley · Grand Prismatic · Old Faithful · Upper Geyser Basin · Mud Volcano return

Day 1 route: Canyon Village → south through Hayden Valley → Fairy Falls TH → Old Faithful → return north via Mud Volcano · ~110 miles round trip

🗺️ Open Full Day Route in Google Maps
Morning — Depart 9:00am (Ideal Timing)
  • 9:00am depart Canyon Village heading south. 9am is actually optimal — less steam at Grand Prismatic by the time you arrive, most vivid colors.
  • Drive slowly south through Hayden Valley — windows down, speed 15 mph, scan slopes. 8–11am is a productive secondary wildlife window for grizzlies. You'll pass through Hayden both directions today.
Grand Prismatic — Use the Fairy Falls Trailhead
  • Do NOT park at the main Midway Geyser Basin lot — fills by 9–10am with no overflow. Fairy Falls TH has two lots, always available.
Grand Prismatic Overlook Trail
1.2 mi RT Easy ~100 ft gain 45–60 min NPS: Easy · 1–2 hrs
AllTrails ↗
📍 Open in Google Maps

Gradual uphill from Fairy Falls TH to the overlook. At the top: the aerial rainbow view — brilliant blue center (240°F water), orange/red/yellow rings of thermophilic bacteria in cooling zones. This is the most iconic image in all of Yellowstone.

9:30am arrival = ideal. Early morning steam obscures the colors. By 9:30–10am the steam has lifted and the blues and oranges are most vivid. You're arriving at the perfect time.

Stay on the boardwalk at all times. The crust over geothermal features can be inches thick over boiling water. Off-trail deaths have occurred. No exceptions for photos or any other reason.
  • After the overlook, do the Midway Geyser Basin boardwalk (1.0 mi loop, flat, accessible) from the Midway lot — ground-level Prismatic up-close. Pair them together: overlook first, boardwalk second.
Midday — Old Faithful
  • Drive south to Old Faithful (~20 min from Midway). Arrive ~noon.
  • Check predicted eruption times on the board at the Visitor Center immediately on arrival.
  • Watch from the Old Faithful Inn balcony (second floor, outdoor, no room fee). Better sightline than the crowded boardwalk below.
  • Erupts every ~90 minutes. Average eruption: 1–5 minutes.
Observation Point Loop
1.6 mi RT Moderate ~160 ft gain 1–1.5 hrs NPS: Moderate · 1–3 hrs
AllTrails ↗
📍 Open in Google Maps

Switchbacks up the hillside above Old Faithful to a commanding elevated bird's-eye view of the entire Upper Geyser Basin — Old Faithful below you, the geyser field extending in every direction. Far fewer people than the boardwalk. Optional — do this instead of the boardwalk crowd if you have energy.

The best time to do this: climb up before the next eruption, be at the overlook when Old Faithful goes off below you. Use the predicted eruption board to time your ascent.
  • After Old Faithful: walk the Upper Geyser Basin loop (2 hrs, boardwalk) — Castle Geyser, Grand Geyser, Riverside Geyser, Morning Glory Pool. More active geysers per square mile than anywhere on Earth. Don't leave in the traffic wave immediately after the eruption. Walk the basin first, then leave.
Return North — Mud Volcano Add-On
  • Drive back north toward Canyon. Stop at Mud Volcano on the way — it's right on the route.
Mud Volcano Trail
1.5 mi loop Easy* ~50 ft gain 45–60 min NPS: Easy, some steep sections
AllTrails ↗
📍 Open in Google Maps

Loop past turbulent mudpots, hissing steam vents, and a landscape that looks like it belongs on another planet. Dragon's Mouth Spring makes an audible sound like breathing. Mud Caldron is one of the most visually chaotic thermal features in the park. Worth every minute.

Evening — Hayden Valley Return
  • 5–7pm: Drive north through Hayden Valley one more time heading back to Canyon. This is the evening wildlife window — second chance for grizzly sightings. The day started and ended in Hayden. That's intentional.

Lamar Valley — Choose Your Approach
Option A · Wolves (4:30am Departure)
Bed by 9:30pm → 4:00am alarm → call Dunraven → 4:30am depart. Arrive Lamar 5:30–6am dawn window. Wolves, grizzlies, bison — the full picture. No other timing makes wolf sightings realistic.
Option B · 9:00am Departure
Normal morning. Arrive Lamar ~10:30am. Bison: guaranteed. Grizzly: realistic. Wolves: unlikely but not impossible. Honest expectation: a great wildlife drive, just not a wolf safari.
At Lamar Valley
  • Drive slowly east through the valley. Any car pulled over with spotting scopes = stop at the next pullout and look in the same direction. That's the system.
  • Wolf packs to look for: Junction Butte pack and Lamar Canyon pack. Best sightings near Slough Creek pullout and Lamar River Bridge.
  • Bison: the top 2 Yellowstone TikTok videos (29M + 24M views) are just bison on this road. Budget 20+ extra minutes for jams.
Trout Lake Trail
1.2 mi RT Easy ~100 ft gain 45–60 min
AllTrails ↗
📍 Open in Google Maps

Short uphill climb to a crystal-clear lake in an open meadow bowl. In late June, cutthroat trout are visibly spawning — you can see them from the bank. Northern river otters use this lake. A quiet, easy stop after the intensity of Lamar wildlife watching.

Trailhead is on the north side of the road ~1 mile west of the Pebble Creek Campground turnoff. Easy to miss — watch for the small pullout and trailhead sign.
Afternoon — Canyon Rims: Thorough Pass
  • Return to Canyon Village by 12:30–1pm (early if Option A dawn departure). You're back at your base — use the afternoon for Canyon in full.
  • If you did the arrival evening and yesterday morning you've only seen Artist Point. This afternoon: do the full canyon circuit.
Uncle Tom's Trail
~0.5 mi RT Strenuous* 328 steel steps 20–30 min
AllTrails ↗
📍 Open in Google Maps

328 open-grate steel steps bolted directly into the canyon wall. Descends to the closest accessible view of the Lower Falls — you feel the mist, hear the thunder, look straight up at 308 feet of waterfall. Short but intense. Not for height-sensitive visitors.

The steps are open-grate (you can see down through them). If heights are an issue, Artist Point gives a nearly equivalent view without the exposure.
Brink of the Lower Falls Trail
0.8 mi RT Steep descent 600 ft drop 30–45 min NPS: Steep descent
AllTrails ↗
📍 Open in Google Maps

Ten paved switchbacks descend 600 ft to the exact lip of the 308-ft Lower Falls. Stand at the brink — water rushes past your feet, mist rises, the roar is visceral. One of the most powerful viewpoints in the park.

⚠️ North Rim Drive is one-way. Once you enter, you cannot reverse. Plan the drive direction accordingly.

  • Inspiration Point — short walk from a pullout on North Rim Drive. Dramatic downstream canyon view at golden hour. Best of the day if the timing works out.
  • South Rim Trail (2.0 mi one-way from Chittenden Bridge to Artist Point) — walk it instead of driving. The trail hugs the rim with continuous canyon views that are better than from the car.
Evening

  • Pack tonight. Checkout is 11am tomorrow.

Bison Jam Protocol
The #1 and #2 Yellowstone videos on TikTok are just bison walking on a road. It will happen to you. This is how it works.
Rule 1
Stay in the vehicle. Do not get out for a closer shot.
Rule 2
Give right of way. They move on their own in 10–20 minutes.
Rule 3
Do not honk. Move slowly if space opens ahead of you.
Rule 4
Budget 20+ extra minutes on every Hayden and Lamar Valley drive.
Distances
25 yards from bison + elk. 100 yards from bears + wolves. Always.

From the Park — Insider Tips
Sourced from Yellowstone National Park Lodges · Things most visitors don't do
Tip 1
Yellowstone Lake — The Quiet Alternative
While everyone crowds Old Faithful at midday, the 136-square-mile lake (largest high-elevation lake in North America) is quiet. Scenic cruises, guided fishing, and boat rentals available. A natural afternoon complement to the morning geyser basin.
Tip 2
Stargazing After Dark
Walk 100 yards from your campsite after dark. Very little light pollution across 2.2M acres. June nights are cold but clear. Bring a layer, download a star app before you lose signal, and spend 20 minutes looking up. There's nothing else like it at 7,900 ft.
Tip 3
Old Faithful Inn Balcony — For Everyone
The Old Faithful Inn is a National Historic Landmark (1903). The second-floor outdoor balcony is open to all visitors — no room, no fee. When the boardwalk crowd is elbow-to-elbow, go up. Better angle, more breathing room, same eruption.
Tip 4
West Thumb Geyser Basin — Underrated
Boiling pools directly on the shore of Yellowstone Lake. Equal visual drama to Midway Geyser Basin, significantly fewer crowds. Great for the departure morning if you route south, or as an add-on anytime you're near Lake Village.
Tip 5
98% of the Park is Off the Roads
Even a half-mile trail completely changes the experience. The moment you step away from pavement, the crowds vanish. The park looks, sounds, and feels different when you're not in a vehicle. Every trail on this guide delivers that shift within minutes.
Tip 6
~50 Miles Between Major Attractions
Old Faithful to Canyon: ~50 miles. Factor in road construction delays on every segment. The park is genuinely large — don't assume things are close because they're "all in Yellowstone." Your two full days are designed around this reality.
What Not to Do
Rules that exist because of real incidents — not suggestions
🚷
Do Not Leave Boardwalks or Designated Trails
The ground over geothermal features can be inches thick over boiling water. The surface looks solid. It is not always solid. People have died going off-trail here. The boardwalk boundary is absolute. Supervise children at all times near thermal areas. Dogs, bikes, and scooters prohibited on boardwalks.
🐻
Maintain Distance from All Wildlife
100 yards from bears, wolves, and nesting birds. 25 yards from all other wildlife. If an animal changes its behavior because of your presence — stops, moves away, looks at you — you are too close and must back up immediately. Bull bison and elk cows with calves are unpredictable at any distance.
📴
Do Not Rely on Cell Service for Navigation
Minimal coverage across 2.2M acres and 350+ miles of road. Download the NPS app for offline use before leaving cell range. Download Google Maps offline. Carry the physical park map from the entrance gate. GPS works without internet — offline maps do not require signal.
🪨
Do Not Remove Anything from the Park
Rocks, antlers, thermal deposits, flowers — all prohibited. With millions of annual visitors, even small removals degrade the park. Take photos. It's not a small rule — it's a federal violation.
