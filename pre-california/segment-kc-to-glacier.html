Chimney Rock & Scotts Bluff National Monument
US-26 north of I-80, western Nebraska Free w/ NPS Pass 1.5 hrs total

Chimney Rock rises 300+ ft above the North Platte valley floor — the single most-cited landmark in every Oregon, California, and Mormon Trail pioneer diary. Every wagon train heading west saw it as a navigation marker for weeks before passing it. Visitor center open 9am–4pm MT. Scotts Bluff NM nearby: 800-ft bluff, summit road 9am–5:30pm, views into Wyoming and Colorado.

📍 Chimney Rock 📍 Scotts Bluff NM
Both sites are free with America the Beautiful pass. Western Nebraska is fully exposed — sunscreen mandatory. Split wheel at Scottsbluff (2.5 hrs in).

Homestead Crater — Midway, UT
Midway, UT · off US-40 from I-80 Book Ahead · homesteadresort.com 90–96°F geothermal pool · only warm-water scuba in continental US

A 55-foot dome of travertine limestone enclosing a 90–96°F geothermal pool. Swim in it, scuba dive in it, or just stand inside the cave opening and watch the blue light hit the water. Hours: Mon–Fri 8:30am–10:30pm · Sat–Sun 8am–10:30pm. Midway detour adds ~90 min round trip from I-80.

📍 Open in Google Maps
Bentonite Hills — Hanksville, UT
Hanksville, UT · off Cow Dung Road (past Mars Desert Research Station) Best Time · Cloudy afternoon or blue hour (3–4pm) ⚠️ Clay road — avoid after rain 1–2 hrs photography stop

Otherworldly badlands of rust-red and multi-colored clay bentonite formations. The hills shift from orange to purple to deep red depending on light and angle. Most dramatic from the air or during golden hour — bluebird skies wash out colors. Optimal light: cloudy afternoon (3–4pm) or blue hour (dusk). The road is clay-based and treacherous after precipitation — check weather before committing.

Navigation caveat: Google Earth's "Bentonite Hills" marker points to the wrong location. These are the hills off Cow Dung Road, just past the Mars Desert Research Station outside Hanksville. Have a detailed map or offline route ready.

Salt Lake City — Red Iguana Lunch
736 W North Temple, SLC, UT 4.6⭐ · 10,374 reviews · legendary mole 45–60 min stop

The city backed by 11,000-ft Wasatch peaks is one of the most dramatic urban settings in America. Red Iguana (736 W North Temple) is the legendary mole restaurant — 30-ingredient mole negro, expect a line, worth every minute. SLC pass-through, not overnight.

Temple Square is a 5-minute walk from Red Iguana — free outdoor grounds, impressive architecture regardless of your beliefs.

📍 Red Iguana 📍 Temple Square
SLC's I-15/I-80 interchange congests 3–6pm — pass through before 3pm or after 6pm.
Bonneville Salt Flats
Wendover, UT · I-80 Exit 4 Free 30,000 acres white · land speed records

You step out of the car onto a surface that looks like a frozen white planet. 30,000 acres of perfectly flat salt crust. Drive on it. Walk on it. The salt reflects UV upward — sunscreen under your chin and nose. June is peak crust condition before summer heat cracks the patterns. The ThrustSSC broke the sound barrier here at 763 mph in 1997. ⚠️ Check usfra.org for any race week closures around June 20.

Shoshone Falls — Twin Falls, ID
Twin Falls, ID · US-93 North from Wendover 212 ft drop · taller than Niagara $5/vehicle · peak June flow

The Snake River drops 212 feet over a 900-foot-wide basalt ledge — taller than Niagara by 36 feet. June is the best month: full snowmelt flow before irrigation diversions begin. The overlook is a 3-minute walk. Spray is heavy enough to feel from 100 feet away. The Perrine Bridge nearby (480 ft above the canyon) allows year-round BASE jumping without a permit — you'll likely see jumpers.

Salmon River Scenic Byway — River of No Return
US-93 North from Twin Falls · ~3.5 hrs driving Free · On-Route 100+ mi canyon · No cell service

US-93 enters a landscape most Americans have never seen. The Snake River canyon gives way to central Idaho high country, then the Salmon River — the River of No Return — carves 100+ miles through vertical rhyolite cliffs. June: the river runs jade-green and high from snowmelt. Bald eagles, bighorn sheep on cliff faces, river otters in pools, spawning salmon leaping in shallows — visible from the road. Zero cell service for ~80 miles. Download offline maps in Twin Falls.

📍 Salmon River Canyon
Two-lane mountain highway — follow the river, don't push pace. Watch for cattle near Elk Bend and Challis. You'll get behind RVs. It doesn't matter. This road is driven slowly on purpose.
CSKT National Bison Range — Red Sleep Mountain Drive
Moiese, MT · US-93 via Ronan ⚠️ Gate closes 6pm sharp 19-mile drive · 1.5–2 hrs 350 bison + pronghorn + elk + bighorn

18,800 acres of native Palouse grassland managed by the Confederated Salish and Kootenai Tribes. The 19-mile Red Sleep Mountain Drive puts you at close range with the herd on open hillside. June: bison calves ("red dogs") born April–May are visible alongside the main herd. Note: does NOT accept America the Beautiful pass — separate fee required. Gate closes at 6pm sharp — enter by 5:45pm maximum.

📍 National Bison Range
From Twin Falls to the Bison Range is ~3.5 hrs. Arriving by 4pm gives a comfortable 1.5-hr drive window before gate closes. Depart Twin Falls by 7–7:30am to make this work.
Flathead Lake + Polson, MT
Polson, MT · US-93 north of Ronan Free · Pass-Through Largest freshwater lake west of Mississippi

30 miles long, glacier-cold, crystal clear, absurdly blue against the Mission Mountains to the east. US-93 runs along the east shore for 15 miles — pull off at any overlook. Montana residents consider this the crown jewel of the state.

West Glacier + Lake McDonald — First Look
West Glacier, MT · West Entrance $35/vehicle 7-day park pass 45–60 min stop

Lake McDonald: 10-mile turquoise glacial lake rimmed in old-growth cedar and hemlock. The shoreline stones are multicolored — red, green, blue — from iron and mineral staining, polished by glaciers. Drive the Going-to-the-Sun Road as far as you like. This is the orientation. You have three full days starting tomorrow.

📍 Lake McDonald
Marias Pass → St. Mary → Johnson's Check-In
  • Marias Pass (5,213 ft) — the lowest northern Rockies Continental Divide crossing, used by the Great Northern Railroad in 1891. At the summit, the Rockies open east into flat grassland. One of the most dramatic landscape transitions in North America.
  • St. Mary Visitor Center (8am–4:30pm): stop before anything else — confirm Many Glacier shuttle status for June 24, pick up trail maps, get current road and trail conditions from rangers.
  • Check into Johnson's of St. Mary. CONFIRMED reservation June 22–25. Open the glacier-guide.html for the full 4-day plan.
