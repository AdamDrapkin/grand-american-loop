🗺️ Open Day 5 Route in Google Maps
Woodland Park, CO — Skyline Drive Mountain Town
Woodland Park, CO · US-24 corridor 881K Instagram views across Skyline Drive route Mountain town scenic overlook + cafe culture 45–60 min stop

Gateway between Colorado Springs sprawl and alpine mountain scenery. Woodland Park sits at 8,850 ft with views east toward the plains and west toward Pikes Peak. The town has galleries, coffee shops, and authentic mountain hospitality. This is where the Front Range elevation transition becomes dramatic — you're moving from urban to alpine landscape in under 2 hours from Denver.

📍 Woodland Park
Stop for coffee and a moment. The Woodland Park Town Hall area has good views west. Peak engagement on Skyline Drive happens through this corridor — the elevation change from plains to mountains drives all the Instagram engagement.
Manitou Springs, CO — Highest Per-Reel Engagement
Manitou Springs, CO · Highway 24 west approach HIGHEST PERFORMANCE 867K views · 86.7K average per reel Adventure gateway + mineral springs + hiking

The Manitou Incline, Manitou Springs mineral hot springs, and the sacred geography of Pikes Peak approach create content that resonates deeply with adventure travelers. Even though fewer total reels exist here than other stops, each reel averages the highest views-per-post of anywhere on the entire Skyline Drive. Stop for water, stretch, and take in the town's bohemian mountain culture.

📍 Manitou Springs
Rest stop: cafes and mineral water available. If you have 15 minutes, the Manitou Springs town square (downtown area) has scenic views south toward the canyon. This stop sets up your entry into the Maroon Bells corridor.
Aspen, CO — Mountain Town Luxury Aesthetic
Aspen, CO · 39.19 N, -106.82 W MAJOR DESTINATION 3.45M views · Resort + alpine town culture Verified creators: @austinfriesen, @aspenco 1–2 hours walkable downtown

Aspen is where the Skyline Drive corridor transitions into the Maroon Bells alpine experience. The town sits at 8,000 ft at the base of 14,000-ft peaks. Main Street has galleries, restaurants, and upscale mountain hospitality. Every influencer traveling Colorado has content from Aspen — the aesthetic of luxury mountain living creates the visual hook that drives 3.45M views.

📍 Aspen Town Square
Lunch or coffee break here. Park downtown and walk the galleries and shops. The mountain backdrop with elegant town architecture is the reason this location gets so much Instagram engagement — it's aspirational mountain living.
Mt. Massive Scenic Drive — VIRAL ENGAGEMENT PEAK
Mt. Massive area, CO · 39.19 N, -106.38 W HIGHEST ON ENTIRE ROUTE 9.26M VIEWS · Single location record Mega-influencers: Donut Media (1.77M), Condé Nast Traveler (3.45M) Alpine peaks + scenic drive corridor

Mt. Massive is Colorado's 2nd-highest peak (14,428 ft) and the scenic drive corridor approaching it is where the mega-influencers focus their content. The views of multiple 14,000-ft peaks from the highway, the elevation drama, and the raw alpine landscape create compelling visuals. This single location carries more Instagram engagement than entire routes on other days.

📍 Mt. Massive Area
Drive slowly through this section. Pull over at scenic turnouts. The engagement data shows that people stop here, take photos from the highway, and share. This is the highest-performing scenic corridor on your entire Colorado route.
Glenwood Springs, CO — Golden Hour Critical Timing
Glenwood Springs, CO · 39.55 N, -107.32 W CRITICAL ARRIVAL: 6–7:30 PM 1.93M views · Hot springs + town + canyon ⚠️ Golden hour sunset timing is ESSENTIAL for engagement

Glenwood Springs is your scenic stop before Echo Canyon. The town sits where the Colorado River emerges from Glenwood Canyon — dramatic geology, geothermal hot springs, and surrounding peaks create a natural amphitheater for photography. Arriving in the 6–7:30 PM golden hour window is critical: the sun hits the canyon walls, the light turns red-orange, and the reflections in the river and mineral water are at peak beauty. This timing drives the 1.93M Instagram views.

📍 Glenwood Springs Hot Springs 📍 Glenwood Canyon Overlook
TIME YOUR ARRIVAL FOR 6–7:30 PM. Walk the river front, look at the geothermal pools, and get to a canyon overlook by sunset. The golden hour transforms this location — the canyon walls glow red, the water reflects the sky. This is why it gets 1.93M views. Plan to arrive by 6 PM to maximize the window.

The Crater at Homestead — Midway, UT ✅
700 N Homestead Drive, Midway UT 84049 CONFIRMED ✅ · Swim · 11:30 AM – 12:30 PM · Jun 20 2 guests · $84.76 · Conf: 8a821ba9723 90–96°F geothermal pool · only warm-water scuba in continental US

A 55-foot dome of travertine limestone enclosing a 90–96°F geothermal pool. Swim in it, scuba dive in it, or just stand inside the cave opening and watch the blue light hit the water. Changing areas and storage inside the Crater. Restrooms in the building just south of the Activity Center.

Salt Lake City — Red Iguana Lunch
736 W North Temple, SLC, UT 4.6⭐ · 10,374 reviews · legendary mole 45–60 min stop

The city backed by 11,000-ft Wasatch peaks is one of the most dramatic urban settings in America. Red Iguana (736 W North Temple) is the legendary mole restaurant — 30-ingredient mole negro, expect a line, worth every minute. SLC pass-through, not overnight.

Temple Square is a 5-minute walk from Red Iguana — free outdoor grounds, impressive architecture regardless of your beliefs.

📍 Red Iguana 📍 Temple Square
SLC's I-15/I-80 interchange congests 3–6pm — pass through before 3pm or after 6pm.
Bonneville Salt Flats
Wendover, UT · I-80 Exit 4 Free 30,000 acres white · land speed records

You step out of the car onto a surface that looks like a frozen white planet. 30,000 acres of perfectly flat salt crust. Drive on it. Walk on it. The salt reflects UV upward — sunscreen under your chin and nose. June is peak crust condition before summer heat cracks the patterns. The ThrustSSC broke the sound barrier here at 763 mph in 1997. ⚠️ Check usfra.org for any race week closures around June 20.

Shoshone Falls + Perrine Bridge — Twin Falls, ID
Twin Falls, ID · US-93 North from Wendover 212 ft drop · taller than Niagara $5/vehicle · closes at sunset · June sunset 9–10pm ⚠️ America the Beautiful pass NOT valid — city park, not federal 35 min total · Perrine Bridge 5 min by car

The Snake River drops 212 feet over a 900-foot-wide basalt ledge — taller than Niagara by 36 feet. June is the best month: full snowmelt flow before irrigation diversions begin. The overlook is a 3-minute walk from the parking lot. Spray is heavy enough to feel from 100 feet away. This is the whole experience — 20 minutes is enough.

Perrine Bridge — 5 minutes by car. A 486-foot span above the Snake River Canyon where BASE jumping is legal year-round, no permit required. On a Saturday evening in June you will almost certainly watch someone step off the edge. Pull over, watch one jump, take the photo, leave. Add 15 minutes.

📍 Shoshone Falls 📍 Perrine Bridge
Closes at sunset — confirmed by Twin Falls Parks. June 20 sunset is approximately 9:15 PM MDT, so the park stays open until 9–10 PM. A 7:45 PM arrival gives you a full hour of light with zero time pressure.

Salmon River Scenic Byway — River of No Return
US-93 North from Twin Falls · ~3.5 hrs driving Free · On-Route 100+ mi canyon · No cell service

US-93 enters a landscape most Americans have never seen. The Snake River canyon gives way to central Idaho high country, then the Salmon River — the River of No Return — carves 100+ miles through vertical rhyolite cliffs. June: the river runs jade-green and high from snowmelt. Bald eagles, bighorn sheep on cliff faces, river otters in pools, spawning salmon leaping in shallows — visible from the road. Zero cell service for ~80 miles. Download offline maps in Twin Falls.

📍 Salmon River Canyon
Two-lane mountain highway — follow the river, don't push pace. Watch for cattle near Elk Bend and Challis. You'll get behind RVs. It doesn't matter. This road is driven slowly on purpose.
CSKT National Bison Range — Red Sleep Mountain Drive
Moiese, MT · US-93 via Ronan ⚠️ Gate closes 6pm sharp 19-mile drive · 1.5–2 hrs 350 bison + pronghorn + elk + bighorn

18,800 acres of native Palouse grassland managed by the Confederated Salish and Kootenai Tribes. The 19-mile Red Sleep Mountain Drive puts you at close range with the herd on open hillside. June: bison calves ("red dogs") born April–May are visible alongside the main herd. Note: does NOT accept America the Beautiful pass — separate fee required. Gate closes at 6pm sharp — enter by 5:45pm maximum.

📍 National Bison Range
From Twin Falls to the Bison Range is ~3.5 hrs. Arriving by 4pm gives a comfortable 1.5-hr drive window before gate closes. Depart Twin Falls by 7–7:30am to make this work.
Flathead Lake + Polson, MT
Polson, MT · US-93 north of Ronan Free · Pass-Through Largest freshwater lake west of Mississippi

30 miles long, glacier-cold, crystal clear, absurdly blue against the Mission Mountains to the east. US-93 runs along the east shore for 15 miles — pull off at any overlook. Montana residents consider this the crown jewel of the state.

West Glacier + Lake McDonald — First Look
West Glacier, MT · West Entrance $35/vehicle 7-day park pass 45–60 min stop

Lake McDonald: 10-mile turquoise glacial lake rimmed in old-growth cedar and hemlock. The shoreline stones are multicolored — red, green, blue — from iron and mineral staining, polished by glaciers. Drive the Going-to-the-Sun Road as far as you like. This is the orientation. You have three full days starting tomorrow.

📍 Lake McDonald
Marias Pass → St. Mary → Johnson's Check-In
  • Marias Pass (5,213 ft) — the lowest northern Rockies Continental Divide crossing, used by the Great Northern Railroad in 1891. At the summit, the Rockies open east into flat grassland. One of the most dramatic landscape transitions in North America.
  • St. Mary Visitor Center (8am–4:30pm): stop before anything else — confirm Many Glacier shuttle status for June 24, pick up trail maps, get current road and trail conditions from rangers.
