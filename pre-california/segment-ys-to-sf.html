Before You Leave — Final Yellowstone
  • Artist Point — one last time. Morning light on the canyon walls is different every time. 5 minutes from the campsite. Do it.
  • Checkout Canyon Campground by 11am. Exit via North Entrance at Gardiner, MT.
  • ⚠️ Budget 15 minutes at the Gardner River High Bridge — active construction, single-lane 24/7 delays through October 2026. Factor this into departure time.
The Drive — I-90 West Through Montana
  • I-90 follows the Clark Fork River through forested mountain valleys — genuinely beautiful through Butte and Missoula.
  • Wheel swap at Missoula (~3 hrs in) — TA at I-90 Exit 96, full facilities, natural break point. This is what "split the wheel" means: Driver 1 takes Yellowstone → Missoula, Driver 2 takes Missoula → Spokane.
  • Coeur d'Alene, ID section of I-90 has lake views directly from the highway — worth slowing down for.
Spokane Evening
  • After 500 miles you won't want much. But Riverfront Park (5 min from I-90, downtown) is worth a 30-minute walk — the Spokane River cut a dramatic 100-ft gorge right through the city center, there's a gondola over it, and the park is genuinely pleasant in the evening.
  • Spokane has solid downtown restaurants within walking distance of the park.

🗺️ Spokane → Paradise Route 📍 Paradise Visitor Center
The Drive Up — SR-706 to Paradise
  • From Spokane, I-90 West → exit south toward Puyallup/Tacoma → SR-7 through Elbe → SR-706. This 18-mile climb through old-growth fir forest to treeline is the first payoff — the drive itself is spectacular.
  • Allow 45 minutes from Elbe to Paradise. Not 30. Two-lane road with RV traffic. Worth the pace.
  • Check the webcam before committing: visitrainier.com — if the mountain is socked in, skip and add time in Seattle. On a clear day, Rainier fills the entire sky from Paradise and you'll know it was worth every mile.
At Paradise — What to Do
Alta Vista Trail — Recommended
1.7 mi RT Moderate 600 ft gain 1.5 hrs Best in late June

Climbs from the Paradise parking area up into open subalpine meadow with Rainier's glaciers filling the sky directly above and the Tatoosh Range below. In late June 2026 with record-low snowpack, the wildflowers are starting and more of the trail is clear than a typical year. This is the move if you have 2 hours.

The trail starts right from the Jackson Visitor Center parking lot. No shuttle needed, no gear beyond trail shoes and a layer.

📍 Alta Vista Trailhead
Start early — cloud typically builds over the summit by early afternoon. The 9am–11am window gives the clearest views and lightest crowds. Be at the Paradise parking lot by 9:30am for the best experience.
Myrtle Falls + Visitor Center Loop — Easier Option
0.6 mi RT Easy · Paved 30–40 min All ages

A paved path from the Visitor Center to Myrtle Falls — a beautiful small waterfall framing the volcano above it. The classic Rainier photo. Combine with a walk through the meadow boardwalk and you've seen the essence of Paradise in 40 minutes without significant elevation gain.

📍 Myrtle Falls
  • Paradise Inn (1917, National Historic Landmark, open June–September) — massive lodge built entirely from salvaged old-growth timber. The lobby has a 33-ft fireplace built from local stone. Get coffee here. 10 minutes inside, no charge. One of the finest park lodges in the NPS system.
📍 Paradise Inn

Route Logic — Why Rainier Before Seattle
  • From Spokane, I-90 deposits you just east of Seattle. Rainier is 60 miles south near Tacoma. Going Seattle first means 3 hours of backtracking (Seattle → Rainier → Seattle again). Going Rainier first is zero backtracking: Spokane → Rainier → drive north to Seattle naturally.
Pike Place Market + Chowder — Seattle
Pike Place Market, Seattle · 2 hrs

Pike Place Chowder (1530 Post Alley, in Pike Place Market) — 4.6⭐ / 8,022 reviews, multiple "best chowder in America" awards. New England clam chowder in a sourdough bread bowl. Opens 11am. Arrive right when they open to avoid the line.

After chowder: fish-throwing at the main market stalls, the original 1971 Starbucks (Pike Place), and the Market's lower levels (crafts, produce, odd shops). The market is genuinely alive — 30–60 minutes exploring feels right.

📍 Rim Village 🗺️ Portland → Crater Lake
What Crater Lake Actually Is
  • Mt. Mazama — a 12,000-ft volcano — collapsed inward after a massive eruption approximately 7,700 years ago. The caldera filled with rain and snowmelt over centuries. No rivers flow in or out. The result is the deepest lake in the United States (1,943 ft) with water so clear and so deep it absorbs all light except deep blue. The color is the real thing. It photographs poorly — you need to see it.
  • Wizard Island is a cinder cone that formed after the eruption — a volcano inside the collapsed volcano, rising 763 ft from the lake surface. Visible from the rim at all times.
  • You're arriving evening. The lake changes color as the sun drops — vivid blue to deep indigo. This is intentionally the right time to be here.
The Plan — Evening at the Rim
Discovery Point Trail — Best Western Rim Viewpoint
1.6 mi RT from Rim Village Easy 150 ft gain 45 min

A rim trail heading west from Rim Village along the caldera edge. Discovery Point was the first place John Wesley Hillman sighted the lake in 1853. The view looks directly across to Wizard Island with the full lake expanse behind it. In evening light the contrast between the dark rim rock and the glowing blue water is extraordinary.

📍 Discovery Point
Walk Discovery Point, then drive the accessible portion of West Rim Drive toward Hillman Peak for a few miles. Pull off at any overlook. Watch the sun drop. Then drive south via OR-62 → I-5 South to Petro Phoenix OR (~1h from the south entrance). Note: no Love's at Grants Pass — Petro Phoenix is the closest verified chain stop.
Rim Village Viewpoint + Sun Notch
Rim Village: immediate · paved Easy Sun Notch: 1.6 mi RT · 150 ft gain · 45 min

Rim Village viewpoint is right from the parking lot — paved, accessible, the lake in front of you immediately on arrival. The gift shop and Rim Village Café are here. The Sinnott Memorial Overlook (short paved walk from the village) has a wider angle on the lake including Wizard Island.

Sun Notch Trail is the alternate trail if Discovery Point is crowded — 1.6 miles RT heading east from the Annie Spring entrance, with a viewpoint of the lake and Phantom Ship (a natural rock formation that looks like a 19th-century sailing ship). Equally vivid.

📍 Rim Village
Cleetwood Cove Closed 2026–2028
The only trail to the water's edge is closed for geotechnical rehabilitation. All boat tours and Wizard Island access are suspended through 2028. Rim-only visit. The rim is still the main event.
East Rim Drive — Call First
East Rim Drive opens late June / early July depending on snowpack. Call 541-594-3000 the morning of June 30 to confirm. West Rim Drive from Rim Village is reliably open.

Multnomah Falls — Columbia River Gorge
I-84 Exit 31 · on the way to Portland Free viewing 620 ft · 30–45 min

Most-visited natural site in Oregon. 620-ft two-tier waterfall visible from the parking area. The paved path to Benson Bridge (0.5 mi, 180-ft gain) puts you between the two tiers with the upper falls above and lower falls below. Quick stop on the I-84 corridor before Portland.

📍 Multnomah Falls
Portland — Voodoo Doughnut + Powell's Books
Portland, OR · 1–1.5 hrs

Powell's Books (1005 W Burnside) — the largest independent bookstore in the world, size of a city block. Even 20 minutes here is worthwhile.

🗺️ Open Day 17 Route in Google Maps
Jedediah Smith Redwoods — Howland Hill Road
9 miles east of Crescent City on US-199 Howland Hill Rd: 6 mi unpaved · no trailers $8/vehicle picnic areas

The densest old-growth coast redwood groves anywhere. The canopy is so thick the forest floor stays dim at midday. Howland Hill Road (6 miles unpaved, no trailers) is the most intimate old-growth redwood drive in the park system — you're inside the grove, trees pressing in from both sides, sunlight barely reaching the forest floor through 350-ft canopies.

The drive from Grants Pass via US-199 through the Smith River canyon is itself part of the experience — tight switchbacks through deep gorge with zero cell service for ~80 miles. Download offline maps in Grants Pass before turning west.

📍 Jedediah Smith Redwoods 📍 Howland Hill Road entrance
Howland Hill Road starts near the Hiouchi Visitor Center. Allow 1 hour for the 6-mile road — it's slow and you'll stop constantly. No trailers, no RVs. Standard vehicles are fine. The road reconnects to US-199 on the west end.
Avenue of the Giants — Humboldt Redwoods SP
US-101 alternate · south of Eureka, CA No fee · on-route 31-mile scenic alternate

The 31-mile Avenue of the Giants runs through Humboldt Redwoods State Park directly on your US-101 route south. Enter from the north end for the largest trees. Founders Grove and the Drury Scenic Parkway are the highlights — the trees here are so tall and close together that standing among them feels like being inside a cathedral built at geological scale.

No gas stations on the Avenue — fuel in Fortuna before turning off US-101. Clear Eureka before 3pm to stay ahead of any traffic building south.
