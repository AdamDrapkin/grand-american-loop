17-Mile Drive
Pacific Grove Gate entry · $12.25/vehicle 45 min–1 hr driving loop Exit: Carmel Gate

The most iconic coastal drive on the Monterey Peninsula — private road through Pebble Beach's coastline. Key stops: Ghost Tree (Cypress tree on a rock point, photographed more than almost anything in California), Lone Cypress (the trademark 250-year-old tree on a granite point — the most photographed tree in North America), Carmel Bay viewpoints (clear water, sea otters visible).

Drive slowly. Stop at every pullout that interests you. Exit through the Carmel Gate at the south end — Carmel-by-the-Sea is immediately outside.

📍 17-Mile Drive
Carmel-by-the-Sea
Ocean Avenue · Main village Free 45 min–1 hr

One of the most distinctive villages on the California coast — no chain stores, no streetlights, no house numbers. Ocean Avenue leads from the village to Carmel Beach (white sand, cypress trees, dogs allowed). The entire town is cottages, galleries, wine tasting rooms, and bakeries.

Clinton Walker House ("Butterfly House") at Scenic Rd — private residence designed by Frank Lloyd Wright in 1951. View from the street only. Low-slung organic form growing out of the rocks above the water — his only house on the California coast.

@globetrottingsu (195K, 119,337 views) · @wellnesstravelled (611K, 17,064 views): "Real life Hansel and Gretel"

📍 Ocean Ave, Carmel 📍 Carmel Beach
Carmel Scenic Road Walkway
Scenic Rd, Carmel-by-the-Sea (along the bluff edge) Free 1.5 mi one-way · 30–45 min stroll

The crushed-granite pathway that runs along the bluff edge above Carmel Beach — the most scenic walk in the village. Monterey cypress trees cling to the rocky edge, the Pacific stretches out below, and the white-sand beach is visible the entire length. The path runs south from the end of Ocean Avenue along the cliff above the water. Stunning at any time of day.

📍 Carmel Scenic Road
Carmel Beach Boardwalk
End of Ocean Ave, Carmel-by-the-Sea Free 20–30 min

The wooden boardwalk at the foot of Ocean Avenue leading down to Carmel Beach — white sand, turquoise water, and the Monterey cypress skyline. This is where the main village meets the beach. Dogs are welcome off-leash, which is a signature Carmel feature. The beach is one of the cleanest and most beautiful in California.

📍 Carmel Beach
Carmel Walks
Meeting point: Pine Inn, Ocean Ave at Monte Verde $30/person · Sat & Sun 10:00 AM 2 hrs · Guided walking tour

The original guided walking tour of Carmel-by-the-Sea — 2-hour tours through the village's back lanes, hidden gardens, and notable cottages. The guide shares stories of the literary and artist colony roots (Upton Sinclair, Sinclair Lewis, Ansel Adams all lived here). Highly rated (4.8 on TripAdvisor with 400+ reviews). Runs Saturday and Sunday mornings. Reservations recommended.

Since Day 4 (Jul 5) is a Sunday and you arrive in Carmel ~10 AM, this tour could fit if Point Lobos is pushed to later. Alternatively, self-guided version: pick up the village map at any shop.

Book ahead at carmelwalks.com — the 10 AM Sunday tour is the only one that works with this itinerary. Skip if Point Lobos timing is tight (Point Lobos takes priority).
📍 Pine Inn (Carmel Walks Start)
Carmel Plaza
Ocean Ave at Mission St, Carmel-by-the-Sea Free to browse 20–30 min

An open-air shopping courtyard in the heart of Carmel village — boutique fashion, gourmet shops, wine tasting rooms, and specialty restaurants in a relaxed Mediterranean-style setting. Pet-friendly. Events throughout the year including cooking demonstrations and pop-up markets. Good place for a quick browse or a tasting room stop while walking Ocean Avenue.

Point Lobos State Natural Reserve
⚠️ NOT BOOKED — reservecalifornia.com $10/vehicle China Cove Bird Island Trail · 1 mi flat · 45 min 1–1.5 hrs total

"The greatest meeting of land and sea" (Ansel Adams). Sea lions hauled out on the rocks, sea otters floating in the kelp beds, harbor seals at China Cove. In July you'll see all three. The China Cove Bird Island Trail is the essential loop — turquoise water, seabird colonies on Bird Island, cypress groves.

Book at reservecalifornia.com before the trip — summer weekends fill. Entry is $10/vehicle. Walk-in spots are limited and fill by 9–10 AM on summer weekends.

Book reservecalifornia.com as soon as possible. The reserve closes when full — advance reservation guarantees your entry. China Cove is at the end of the main road (1.5 mi from entrance). Allow 1–1.5 hours total.
📍 Point Lobos State Reserve
Afternoon — Bixby Bridge · Hurricane Point · Pfeiffer Beach
Bixby Creek Bridge
Drive: 19 min · 11.2 mi from Point Lobos Free 15–20 min

The single most photographed bridge in California — 1932 open-spandrel arch bridge, 260 feet above Bixby Creek canyon, ocean in the background. Use the north turnout (pull off before crossing the bridge going south) for the full bridge-canyon-ocean composition. The south turnout is good too but the north is the classic shot.

📍 Bixby Creek Bridge
Hurricane Point
1 min · 0.7 mi from Bixby Free 15 min

The dramatic headland just south of Bixby — pull off CA-1 on the exposed promontory for views looking north (Bixby Bridge visible) and south (Big Sur coast disappearing into the haze). The wind here is relentless and powerful — it's exactly why it's called Hurricane Point. Worth 15 minutes.

📍 Hurricane Point
Pfeiffer Beach — Purple Sand & Keyhole Arch
33 min · 15.3 mi from Hurricane Point $12/vehicle — CASH ONLY Sycamore Canyon Rd · Unmarked turnoff 1–1.5 hrs

The purple sand is real — manganese garnet eroded from the cliffs above stains the sand in gradients of lavender and violet. The Keyhole Arch at the south end of the beach frames the Pacific perfectly; at certain times of year (November–December) the setting sun passes through the arch, but the arch itself is always photogenic.

How to find the turnoff: On CA-1 heading south, look for Sycamore Canyon Road approximately 0.5 miles north of the Big Sur Station (small brown sign, easy to miss). Single-lane road, no large vehicles, no trailers. Standard vehicles are fine. Cash only at the booth.

Cash only at the entrance booth — $12/vehicle. Have it ready. The road narrows to single-lane and is ~2 miles from the turnoff to the beach.
📍 Pfeiffer Beach
McWay Falls — Julia Pfeiffer Burns SP
25 min · 11.9 mi from Pfeiffer Beach $12/vehicle (or park free across CA-1) Overlook Trail: 0.5 mi round trip · Flat · 20 min

An 80-foot waterfall drops directly onto a secluded beach cove — the water hits a turquoise crescent of sand framed by dark rocks and Big Sur forest. One of the most photographed scenes in all of California. The overlook trail is 0.5 miles flat on a paved path — you stand above the cove on a cliff edge with the full composition below you.

@wanderingcreator_meghana (134K, 73,988 views): "One of the most dramatic views in California"

You can park for free across CA-1 on the shoulder and walk in (saves $12). The overlook is accessible from the parking area via a short tunnel under the highway.
📍 McWay Falls
Drive South — Ragged Point & Big Sur Station
Big Sur Station
CA-1, Big Sur, CA 93920 (near Pfeiffer Beach turnoff) Free 5–10 min · Visitor information & maps

The multi-agency visitor information center for the Big Sur coast — staffed by US Forest Service, State Parks, and NPS rangers. Pick up trail maps, current road/closure info, and get current conditions for every stop on the drive. The Pfeiffer Beach turnoff (Sycamore Canyon Road) is just 0.5 miles north of this station — it's the landmark to look for when finding the unmarked beach turnoff.

Stop here briefly to confirm CA-1 conditions south of Big Sur before continuing. Highway 1 closures south of here do happen — rangers will know.
📍 Big Sur Station
Ragged Point
CA-1, Ragged Point, CA (19 mi north of San Simeon) Free · Pull-off viewpoint 15–20 min · On the route between McWay & Elephant Seals

The dramatic southern terminus of the Big Sur wilderness — where the mountains plunge 400 feet straight into the sea. This dramatic clifftop overlook is considered one of the finest viewpoints on the entire California coast: the rocky shore below, sea stacks, and the open Pacific stretching to the horizon. A short trail leads down to a black-sand beach (steep, 20 min one-way). TripAdvisor rates it 4.5 with 350+ reviews.

Ragged Point falls naturally on the drive between McWay Falls and the Elephant Seals — it's 30 miles south of McWay on CA-1, roughly halfway through the 57-mile stretch.

Elephant Seal Vista Point — Piedras Blancas
CA-1, 1.5 mi north of San Simeon Free 30 min · Paved viewing platform

Hundreds of elephant seals hauled out on the beach directly below a paved viewing platform. In July you'll see juveniles and sub-adults molting, body-surfing, and sparring. The bulls (up to 5,000 lbs) are at sea in summer, but the younger animals are vocal and active. The distinctive vocalization carries up the cliff.

This is one of the few places in the world where you can observe elephant seals year-round without a boat. Pull into the parking area off CA-1 — the platform is 50 feet from the parking lot. Free. No reservation needed.
