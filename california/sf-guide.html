Afternoon — Chinatown & North Waterfront
  • Chinatown (Grant Avenue + Stockton Street) — 45 min to 1 hour. America's oldest Chinatown. Dragon Gate at Bush & Grant Ave is the landmark entry. Gold-coin trinkets, incense shops, dim sum bakeries. Stockton St is the working street (produce, live fish) — more authentic than touristy Grant Ave.
Palace of Fine Arts
3601 Lyon St, San Francisco Free 30–45 min

The colonnade and dome over the lagoon — one of the most photogenic structures in SF. Originally built for the 1915 Panama-Pacific Exposition. The reflection in the lagoon in afternoon light is exceptional. Walk the colonnade perimeter, photograph across the water.

📍 Palace of Fine Arts
Powell-Hyde Cable Car
Powell & Market turnaround · Fisherman's Wharf $24/adult all-day Muni passport Unlimited transit all day

Board at Powell & Market Street (the turnaround where they spin the cars manually — worth watching). Ride up and over the hill to Fisherman's Wharf. The Powell-Hyde line goes over the steepest grades — better views than Powell-Mason. The $24 all-day Muni passport covers all buses, streetcars, and cable cars for the rest of the day.

The turnaround at Powell & Market has a line in the morning. Take the turnaround at Fisherman's Wharf (Hyde & Beach) instead — board there to go back into the city with no wait.
📍 Powell & Market Turnaround
Pier 39 — Sea Lions & Waterfront
The Embarcadero at Beach St, San Francisco Free entry 45 min–1 hr

The cable car ends at Hyde & Beach — Pier 39 is a 10-minute walk east along the waterfront. The star attraction is K-Dock on the west side of the pier, where up to 1,000 California sea lions haul out on floating docks year-round. The barking is audible from a block away — completely free to watch from the upper deck.

Clam chowder in a sourdough bread bowl at the wharf crab stands is the definitive SF street food move. Try it here.

Best sea lion viewing is from the west side of Pier 39 looking down at the floating docks. Morning is less crowded. No admission required — just walk in.
📍 Pier 39
Fisherman's Wharf
Jefferson St between Taylor & Hyde, San Francisco Free to walk 30–45 min

SF's most visited waterfront district — the historic fishing wharf stretching from Pier 33 to Ghirardelli Square. Walk Jefferson Street for the original crab stands, souvenir shops, and street performers. The WWII submarine USS Pampanito (Pier 45) is open for self-guided tours (optional, ticketed).

Ghirardelli Square (900 North Point St, west end) — the 1852 chocolate factory converted to shops and restaurants. Free to explore. The original Ghirardelli shop here serves the famous hot fudge sundae.

📍 Fisherman's Wharf 📍 Ghirardelli Square

Twin Peaks Blvd, San Francisco Free 20 min

Panoramic 360° view of the Bay from a short drive south of the Mission. The city grid, the Bay Bridge, and (on clear days) the Golden Gate. Sunset here on arrival day sets the stage for everything that follows. Short walk from the parking lot to the summit.

The Full House House — 1709 Broderick St
1709 Broderick St, San Francisco (Lower Pacific Heights) Free · Sidewalk view only 10–15 min · Private residence

The blue-grey Victorian at 1709 Broderick Street used for the exterior shots in Full House (1987–1995). The Tanner family's house. It is a private residence — view and photograph from the sidewalk only. Just 0.4 miles from the Painted Ladies at Alamo Square, so combine both on the same walk.

Private home — sidewalk only, no knocking or entering. Quick 10-min photo stop on the way to or from Alamo Square.
📍 1709 Broderick St (Full House House)
Mission Dolores Park
Dolores St & 18th St, Mission District, San Francisco Free 30–45 min

SF's most beloved hillside park — sun-filled terraced lawns with sweeping views of the downtown skyline and the Bay. The upper terrace gives a classic elevated view looking north over the city. The surrounding Mission District (Valencia Street, 16th–24th St) is SF's most vibrant neighborhood: colorful murals, taquerias, bookstores, and some of the city's best restaurants. The Mission is also the sunniest neighborhood in SF — shielded from coastal fog by Twin Peaks.

📍 Mission Dolores Park

Golden Gate Park
Bounded by Fulton St, Lincoln Way, Stanyan St & Great Highway Free to enter 1,017 acres · 3+ hrs if seeing all attractions

The third most visited urban park in the United States. Larger than Central Park. Contains the Japanese Tea Garden, California Academy of Sciences, de Young Museum, Conservatory of Flowers, Stow Lake, and Buffalo Paddock. The main meadow (Hellman Hollow) is perfect for a picnic.

📍 Golden Gate Park (Main Drive)
Japanese Tea Garden
75 Hagiwara Tea Garden Dr, Golden Gate Park $13 adults · $8 seniors · Free before 10 AM on Mon/Wed/Fri 1 hr · Oldest public Japanese garden in the US

The oldest operating public Japanese garden in North America — opened 1894. Natural beauty and tranquility: graceful Japanese maples, twisting pines, clipped azaleas, cherry trees, a steep wooden drum bridge, pagodas, and koi ponds. The traditional teahouse serves matcha and fortune cookies (invented here). Go early — it gets crowded by 11 AM.

Admission is free Mon/Wed/Fri before 10 AM. If you're already in the park in the morning, this is the best free window.
📍 Japanese Tea Garden
California Academy of Sciences
55 Music Concourse Dr, Golden Gate Park $40 adults · $35 seniors · $30 youth (12–17) · $25 children (4–11) 3–4 hrs · Opens 9:30 AM Mon–Sat / 11 AM Sun

The world's only combined aquarium, planetarium, rainforest, and natural history museum under one living roof. The centerpiece is Osher Rainforest — a four-story glass dome with free-flying tropical birds and butterflies. The aquarium has a Philippine coral reef tank and a shark lagoon. The planetarium shows are outstanding. Albino alligator lives in the swamp exhibit.

📍 California Academy of Sciences

Alcatraz Island — Audio Tour
Pier 33 Alcatraz Landing · 9:20 AM departure ✅ Booked · Conf. i77487162 Return ~12:00 PM · ~2.5 hrs on island

The cell house audio tour narrated by former guards and prisoners is the core experience — the actual voices describing life inside. Do the full route: cell house, recreation yard, library, D-block (solitary). Plan 2–2.5 hours. The views of the Bay and SF skyline from the island are extraordinary.

Arrive at Pier 33 by 8:35 AM — 45 min before departure. Boarding closes before departure time. QR code valid for the entire party (on one mobile device). No weapons, no oversized bags. Non-refundable.

Bring: Headphones (app-based audio tour), fully charged phone, backpack max 16"×20", closed-toe shoes (the island is uneven). The audio tour app is free — download before leaving the hotel.

Fort Point National Historic Site
201 Marine Dr, San Francisco, CA 94129 Free Admission Fri 10:00 AM–5:00 PM 45–55 min

Built 1853–1861 directly beneath the south tower of the Golden Gate Bridge — the only pre-Civil War brick fort on the West Coast. Standing inside the fort looking straight up at the Golden Gate span above is one of the most surreal viewpoints in SF. The fort itself is fascinating: arched brick casemates, cannon placements, the narrow stairwells.

@modicumofjoy captured this view (377,503 plays, 23,170 likes). The angle looking up at the bridge from the fort's top level is the shot.

📍 Fort Point NHS
Depart Fort Point by ~1:15 PM sharp to maintain the Berkeley timing. Fort Point closes at 5 PM — you have plenty of time with 45–55 min here.
Golden Gate Bridge
Golden Gate Bridge, San Francisco · East sidewalk open daily Free to walk 30–45 min · 1.7 mi round trip across bridge

One of the great engineering achievements of the 20th century — 4,200 feet of Art Deco suspension span, completed 1937. The east sidewalk is open to pedestrians daily from 5 AM to sunset. Walk out to the first tower (0.5 mi) for the views back to the city and down the Bay. The view from Fort Point directly below the south tower looking straight up at the span is one of the most surreal in SF.

The Presidio of San Francisco surrounds the bridge approach — 1,500 acres of former Army post converted to National Park. Coastal trails, beach access, and the historic Main Post. The bridge's south anchorage viewpoint (Battery East) gives the full span composition.

The Golden Gate Bridge toll plaza viewpoint (east side, off Lincoln Blvd) gives the classic full-span photograph for free. No parking needed if you walk from Fort Point.
📍 Golden Gate Bridge Pedestrian Entry
Baker Beach
Gibson Road, Presidio, San Francisco Free · Presidio National Park 30–45 min

A mile-long beach at the foot of serpentine cliffs in the Presidio — with the Golden Gate Bridge looming at the north end of the beach. The composition of the bridge seen from beach level with the Marin headlands behind it is one of the classic SF photographs. Large waves and undertow make it unsafe for swimming, but it's superb for walking and photography.

From Fort Point, Baker Beach is a 5-minute drive (1.5 mi) — these three (Fort Point, GGB, Baker Beach) are all within the Presidio and can be combined efficiently in the same morning.

📍 Baker Beach
Presidio of San Francisco
Arguello Blvd & Moraga Ave, San Francisco (Main Post) Free · National Park Service 1,491 acres · Former US Army post 1776–1994

The Presidio is the park that holds Fort Point, Baker Beach, the Golden Gate Bridge south anchorage, and 24 miles of trails. The historic Main Post (old Army HQ, now NPS visitor center) has excellent exhibits on the Presidio's history from Spanish colonial fort to military base. Lucasfilm and the Yoda Fountain are on the grounds. Crissy Field (the restored tidal marsh and airfield) runs along the bay waterfront with views of Alcatraz and the bridge.

📍 Presidio Visitor Center

UC Berkeley Campus
2200 Piedmont Ave, Berkeley, CA Campanile: $5/person Sproul Plaza: Free ~1 hour · Depart 3:00 PM sharp

Sather Tower (Campanile) — $5/person to ride the elevator to the observation deck. 307 feet. Panoramic views of the Bay, SF skyline, and Berkeley hills. Worth the $5. The carillon plays at 7:50 AM, 12:00 PM, and 6:00 PM — if you're here around noon you'll hear it.

Sproul Plaza — the epicenter of Free Speech Movement history (1964). The steps, the plaza. Walk through, see the energy of the campus. Free.

Bancroft Way and Telegraph Ave: cafes, bookstores, Berkeley street life. Good place for a quick lunch/coffee before the Muir Woods drive.

Leave Berkeley at 3:00 PM sharp. The Muir Woods parking slot is 4:00–4:30 PM and the drive is 65 min with typical Friday afternoon traffic. Missing the slot is a real risk.

Muir Woods National Monument
✅ Booked · 4:00–4:30 PM parking slot $15/person · gomuirwoods.com NPS hours: 8 AM–8 PM (May–Sep) ~2 hours

Old-growth coast redwoods — the closest ancient redwood grove to SF. Cathedral Grove Trail is 1 mile flat on a boardwalk through trees 250–1,000 years old. The canopy closes overhead and the light shifts. This is the warm-up for the giants you'll see more of on the Big Sur drive.

Parking is required in advance (your slot is confirmed). No cell service inside the park — download the offline NPS map before leaving Berkeley. Auto-theft is common in the parking lot — remove all valuables from the car.

Arrive at your parking slot at 4:00–4:15 PM. You have until closing (8 PM) — roughly 2 hours comfortably. The Cathedral Grove and Bohemian Grove loops are the essential trails. No need to rush.
