Morning — Beverly Hills
  • Rodeo Drive (Wilshire Blvd to Santa Monica Blvd) — the 3-block famous luxury shopping street. Free to walk. Flagship stores for everything. The architecture at the north end (the curved two-story retail street) is the classic photo spot.
  • Beverly Hills residential streets north of Sunset Blvd — massive hedged estates, Rolls Royces in driveways, some of the most expensive real estate in the world on streets designed for meandering.
  • The Beverly Hills Hotel "The Pink Palace" (9641 Sunset Blvd) — the iconic pink and green 1912 hotel. The lobby and Polo Lounge are open to non-guests for drinks/food. The banana leaf wallpaper in the Polo Lounge is instantly recognizable.
Midday — Hollywood
Hollywood Walk of Fame
Hollywood Blvd · La Brea Ave to Vine St · 15 blocks Free 1 hr walk

2,700+ stars embedded in the sidewalk honoring entertainers across film, television, music, radio, and theater. Walk the Hollywood Blvd section between La Brea and Vine for the highest concentration. The stars are organized by category (each has a symbol). Look for favorites by name.

📍 Hollywood Walk of Fame
Grauman's Chinese Theatre
6925 Hollywood Blvd, Hollywood, CA Free to view forecourt

The famous forecourt with hand and footprints of over 200 Hollywood celebrities in cement — since 1927. Find the prints of your favorites. The theatre exterior is the grand 1920s Chinese imperial architecture. IMAX screenings inside (optional). The forecourt itself is free to enter and walk.

📍 Grauman's Chinese Theatre
Walt Disney Concert Hall
111 S Grand Ave, Downtown LA Free exterior & gardens 30–45 min

Frank Gehry's stainless steel masterpiece (2003) — billowing curves of brushed steel that look different from every angle and in every light. The rooftop garden (accessible free) has a Blue Ribbon Garden and a view of the surrounding downtown. The interior is open for self-guided tours when no performance is scheduled.

📍 Walt Disney Concert Hall
Evening
  • Sunset Strip (West Hollywood, Sunset Blvd between Crescent Heights and Doheny) — the Whisky a Go Go, Roxy, Rainbow Bar & Grill, the history of rock and roll. Walk a stretch of it after dinner.
  • Little Tokyo (Downtown, 5 min from Disney Hall) — ramen, udon, Japanese grocery stores, Mikawaya ice cream (mochi originator).
  • Grand Central Market (317 S Broadway, Downtown) — 40+ food vendors in a 1917 market hall. Open until 10 PM. DTLA's best casual food hall. Eggslut, Wexler's Deli, Belcampo, Horse Thief BBQ.

Morning — Griffith Observatory
Griffith Observatory
2800 E Observatory Rd, Los Angeles Free Admission Opens 10:00 AM weekdays Planetarium shows: $10/adult 1.5–2 hrs

The city's greatest view point — the 1935 Art Deco observatory on the southern slope of Mount Hollywood with a 360° panorama of the LA basin, the Hollywood Hills, and the Pacific on clear days. The Hollywood Sign is visible from the front lawn looking northwest (this is the most accessible good angle on the sign).

Inside: the Zeiss telescope in the dome (public viewing nights), the Tesla coil, the Foucault pendulum, the triple beam coelostat. Planetarium shows are ticketed ($10/adult) and worth it for the laser show at night — but the building itself is free.

@thisblisslife: "Sunset = best views. Arrive early." — the observatory is at its most dramatic at sunset when the city lights below match the darkening sky. If energy allows, return for the evening.

Drive up or take the DASH Griffith Park shuttle from Vermont/Sunset (free). Parking is limited and fills by noon on weekdays. The Mt. Hollywood Trail (2.5 mi round trip, easy) starts from the observatory and goes to the summit for an even higher view.
📍 Griffith Observatory
Afternoon — Santa Monica · Venice · Abbot Kinney
Santa Monica Pier
200 Santa Monica Pier, Santa Monica, CA Free to walk · Pacific Park rides separate

The western terminus of Route 66 — a sign at the end of the pier marks it. Pacific Park's Ferris wheel and rollercoaster are on the pier (tickets optional). 3rd Street Promenade is 2 blocks east — outdoor pedestrian mall with restaurants, performers, shops.

📍 Santa Monica Pier
Venice Boardwalk + Abbot Kinney
Ocean Front Walk · Venice, CA 5–10 min from Santa Monica Pier Free 1–2 hrs

Venice Boardwalk — Muscle Beach outdoor gym, street performers, roller skaters, fortune tellers, artists, vendors. Chaotic, colorful, uniquely LA. Walk south from the Santa Monica border.

Abbot Kinney Blvd (3–5 min from boardwalk) — Venice's coolest street. Independent boutiques, coffee roasters (Intelligentsia Venice), bookstores, galleries, farm-to-table restaurants. Named "coolest block in America" by GQ. No chains.

Morning — Santa Monica Farmers Market (8 AM–1 PM)
Santa Monica Farmers Market
✅ Works — Saturday only Arizona Ave & 3rd St, Santa Monica Sat 8:00 AM–1:00 PM · Year-round ~$30–50 family

Founded 1991 — one of California's most celebrated certified farmers markets. 40+ organic farm stands, fresh produce, flowers, honey, artisan goods. The Wednesday version is larger but Saturday works for this trip. Top LA chefs shop here Saturday morning. Walk the whole market, get produce for snacks, buy honey, watch the farmers interact with chefs.

📍 Santa Monica Farmers Market
Late Morning / Afternoon — Options
  • ⚠️ Getty Center (getty.edu) — NOT BOOKED. Free admission but timed entry required — book in advance. Hilltop campus connected by aerial tram. World-class collection (Impressionists, decorative arts), Richard Meier travertine architecture, formal gardens, panoramic city views. Allow 2–3 hours.
  • LACMA Urban Light (5905 Wilshire Blvd) — 238 vintage street lamps arranged in a grid, free and accessible 24/7. Iconic LA photo. Takes 20 minutes. The permanent collection inside is also good (admission fee).
  • Koreatown — best Korean BBQ in the US. All-you-can-eat KBBQ lunch is the move. Jongro BBQ or Soowon Galbi are excellent.
  • Olvera Street (125 Paseo de la Plaza, Downtown) — LA's oldest street, original 1820s Mexican marketplace. Adobe buildings, folk art, handmade crafts. Free.
Evening — Final LA Dinner
  • ⚠️ NOT BOOKED — Book 1–2 weeks in advance.
  • Nobu Malibu (22706 PCH, Malibu) — Over-water deck above the Pacific. Japanese-Peruvian fusion. The Black Cod with Miso. Sunset views. Requires advance booking.
  • Bestia (2121 E 7th Pl, Downtown) — Rustic Italian in the Arts District. Pasta, wood-fire meats, excellent wine list. One of the hardest reservations in LA.
  • Republique (624 S La Brea Ave, Hollywood) — Walter Manzke's French bistro. Exceptional all-day restaurant. Great for a group dinner.
  • Craig's (8826 Melrose Ave, West Hollywood) — The celebrity haunt. Classic American food in a red-leather booth restaurant. Fun for a special night.
  • Pack tonight — depart hotel no later than 9:00 AM tomorrow.
