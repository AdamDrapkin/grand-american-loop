Morning — Beverly Hills
  • Rodeo Drive (Wilshire Blvd to Santa Monica Blvd) — the 3-block famous luxury shopping street. Free to walk. Flagship stores for everything. The architecture at the north end (the curved two-story retail street) is the classic photo spot.
  • Beverly Hills residential streets north of Sunset Blvd — massive hedged estates, Rolls Royces in driveways, some of the most expensive real estate in the world on streets designed for meandering.
  • The Beverly Hills Hotel "The Pink Palace" (9641 Sunset Blvd) — the iconic pink and green 1912 hotel. The lobby and Polo Lounge are open to non-guests for drinks/food. The banana leaf wallpaper in the Polo Lounge is instantly recognizable.
Midday — Hollywood
Hollywood Walk of Fame
Hollywood Blvd · La Brea Ave to Gower St · 15 blocks Free 1 hr walk

2,700+ stars embedded in the sidewalk honoring entertainers across film, television, music, radio, and theater. Walk the Hollywood Blvd section between La Brea and Gower for the highest concentration. The stars are organized by category (each has a symbol). Notable locations: Marilyn Monroe's star at 6774 Hollywood Blvd · Michael Jackson's star at 6927 Hollywood Blvd.

📍 Hollywood Walk of Fame
Grauman's Chinese Theatre
6925 Hollywood Blvd, Hollywood, CA Free to view forecourt

The famous forecourt with hand and footprints of over 200 Hollywood celebrities in cement — since 1927. Find the prints of your favorites. The theatre exterior is the grand 1920s Chinese imperial architecture. IMAX screenings inside (optional). The forecourt itself is free to enter and walk.

📍 Grauman's Chinese Theatre
Walt Disney Concert Hall
111 S Grand Ave, Downtown LA Free exterior & gardens 30–45 min

Frank Gehry's stainless steel masterpiece (2003) — billowing curves of brushed steel that look different from every angle and in every light. The rooftop garden (accessible free) has a Blue Ribbon Garden and a view of the surrounding downtown. The interior is open for self-guided tours when no performance is scheduled.

📍 Walt Disney Concert Hall
Afternoon — Downtown LA
  • Little Tokyo (Downtown, 5 min from Disney Hall) — ramen, udon, Japanese grocery stores, Mikawaya ice cream (mochi originator). One of only three official Japantowns in the United States.
  • LA Chinatown (Broadway & Hill St, 5 min from Little Tokyo) — red lanterns, ornate buildings, specialty restaurants. Easy walking distance from Little Tokyo — do both in the afternoon.
  • Grand Central Market (317 S Broadway, Downtown) — 40+ food vendors in a 1917 market hall. Open until 10 PM. Eggslut, Wexler's Deli, Horse Thief BBQ, Sticky Rice — good for a midday snack before dinner.

Morning — Griffith Observatory
Griffith Observatory
2800 E Observatory Rd, Los Angeles Free Admission Opens 10:00 AM weekdays Planetarium shows: $10/adult 1.5–2 hrs

The city's greatest view point — the 1935 Art Deco observatory on the southern slope of Mount Hollywood with a 360° panorama of the LA basin, the Hollywood Hills, and the Pacific on clear days. The Hollywood Sign is visible from the front lawn looking northwest (this is the most accessible good angle on the sign).

Inside: the Zeiss telescope in the dome (public viewing nights), the Tesla coil, the Foucault pendulum, the triple beam coelostat. Planetarium shows are ticketed ($10/adult) and worth it for the laser show at night — but the building itself is free.

@thisblisslife: "Sunset = best views. Arrive early." — the observatory is at its most dramatic at sunset when the city lights below match the darkening sky. If energy allows, return for the evening.

Drive up or take the DASH Griffith Park shuttle from Vermont/Sunset (free). Parking is limited and fills by noon on weekdays. The Mt. Hollywood Trail (2.5 mi round trip, easy) starts from the observatory and goes to the summit for an even higher view.
📍 Griffith Observatory
Hollywood Sign
Best viewed from Griffith Observatory lawn or Lake Hollywood Park Free · No access to the sign itself (restricted property) Best viewing: Griffith Observatory front lawn · 10 min

The 450-foot white letter sign on Mount Lee — each letter 45 feet high, the global symbol of Hollywood glamour since 1923. The sign sits on restricted property and cannot be approached, but several viewpoints provide excellent photo opportunities: (1) Griffith Observatory front lawn — the most accessible and widely used view, looking northwest from the plaza; (2) Lake Hollywood Park (3204 Canyon Lake Dr) — the classic direct-on view that puts the sign front and center; (3) Hollywood & Highland — framed view through the Babylon Court arch, easy and central.

The Observatory lawn gives you the sign AND the city panorama in one stop — do both together. Lake Hollywood Park (10 min drive from the Observatory) has the most direct, unobstructed sign view if you want the classic photo.
📍 Hollywood Sign (Mount Lee) 📍 Lake Hollywood Park
Griffith Park
4730 Crystal Springs Dr, Los Angeles Free · Largest municipal urban wilderness park in the US 4,107 acres · Trails, merry-go-round, pony rides, zoo adjacent

The park that contains the Observatory, the Hollywood Sign approach trails, the LA Zoo, Travel Town Museum, and the Autry Museum of the American West. 4,107 acres of natural terrain — the largest municipal park and urban wilderness area in the United States. The park itself has multiple family-friendly features beyond the Observatory: the Griffith Park Merry-Go-Round (vintage 1926, $3/ride), Pony Rides (weekends), and 53 miles of hiking and horse trails. The Charlie Turner Trailhead near the Observatory gives access to the Brush Canyon Trail (leads to the Hollywood Sign base ridge).

📍 Venice Boardwalk 📍 Abbot Kinney Blvd
Venice Canals Walkway
Dell Ave & Eastern Canal, Venice, CA (3 blocks from boardwalk) Free · Public walkway 30–45 min · 1 mi of waterside paths

The century-old canal network just 3 blocks from the Venice Boardwalk — a completely different world from the busy waterfront. Six canals lined with historic houses, arched bridges, wooden rowboats, and ducks. Built in 1905 by Abbot Kinney to recreate the ambiance of Venice, Italy. The residential canals are quiet, photogenic, and feel like a hidden village tucked inside the city. Linnie Canal Park has a small playground for kids.

TripAdvisor rates it 4.4 with 2,000+ reviews. The contrast between the chaos of the boardwalk and the quiet of the canals makes this one of the most surprising experiences in LA.

From Abbot Kinney Blvd, walk 2 blocks west on Venice Blvd to reach the canals — it's an easy add-on to the boardwalk visit with no driving required.

Santa Monica Farmers Market
✅ Works — Saturday only Arizona Ave & 3rd St, Santa Monica Sat 8:00 AM–1:00 PM · Year-round ~$30–50 family

Founded 1991 — one of California's most celebrated certified farmers markets. 40+ organic farm stands, fresh produce, flowers, honey, artisan goods. The Wednesday version is larger but Saturday works for this trip. Top LA chefs shop here Saturday morning. Walk the whole market, get produce for snacks, buy honey, watch the farmers interact with chefs.

📍 Santa Monica Farmers Market
Morning — Malibu Coast (10 AM–2 PM)
  • Point Dume State Beach (Westward Beach Rd, Malibu) — dramatic 200-ft bluff jutting into the Pacific. Short bluff trail loops the headland with views of Santa Monica Bay, Catalina Island, and migrating gray whales (Dec–Apr). Zuma Beach is directly adjacent for sand time. $10–15 parking at Westward Beach lot.
  • El Matador State Beach (32215 PCH, Malibu) — the most dramatic beach in the LA area. Sea stacks, sea caves, and rock arches rise directly from the sand. Small, parking-limited, reached by a short cliff staircase. Go early in the day for the best light and fewer crowds. Free to visit, $8 parking lot.
  • These beaches are the best coastal scenery in the entire LA area — far better than Santa Monica or Venice. Morning light on the sea stacks is extraordinary.

Getty Center
⚠️ Timed Entry Required — Book at getty.edu 1200 Getty Center Dr, Los Angeles Free Admission · $20 Parking 2–3 hrs · 43 min from Point Dume · Routes API verified

Richard Meier's travertine hilltop campus (1997) with world-class collections of Impressionist paintings, decorative arts, illuminated manuscripts, and photography. The Central Garden (designed by Robert Irwin) and the north overlook terrace give 270° panoramas of the LA basin, the ocean, and the Santa Monica Mountains. Free admission — book timed entry at getty.edu well in advance. Summer Saturday slots fill weeks out.

From Point Dume: 43 min · 30.0 mi via PCH east → I-405 → Getty Center Dr. Arrive by 3 PM for timed entry. Allow 1.5–2 hrs minimum. Leave by 5:30 PM to reach Malibu Farm for 6:30 PM dinner.
