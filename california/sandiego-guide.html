El Matador State Beach
32215 Pacific Coast Hwy, Malibu, CA $8–12/vehicle day use Arrive before 10 AM 1.5–2 hrs

The most dramatic beach in Southern California — sandstone sea stacks, caves you can walk into, natural rock arches, and deep blue Pacific. The descent from the parking lot is steep (about 80 feet down a wooden staircase and sandy path). The rocks are slippery at low tide — wear closed shoes or sandals with grip.

@visitcalifornia (825K, 8,150 views): "High tides and good vibes" — this is the beach that defines SoCal coastal beauty without the crowds of Malibu proper.

Check tide tables before arriving — low tide (accessible caves and rock formations) vs high tide (smaller beach area) changes the experience significantly. Either is beautiful but different.

Newport Beach · Balboa Island
Marine Avenue, Balboa Island, Newport Beach, CA 1–1.5 hrs

Balboa Island — take the short Balboa Island Ferry (cars or walk-on, $1/person) or drive over the bridge. Marine Avenue is the main street: boutiques, ice cream, bakeries. The island is a classic Newport Beach enclave with million-dollar cottages squeezed onto tiny lots.

Balboa Bar — the original frozen vanilla ice cream bar dipped in chocolate, created on this island in 1945. Still made here. Get one. The coconut and sprinkle toppings are the classic.

Newport Beach Pier — the commercial harbor has the largest recreational sport fishing fleet in the West Coast. Walk the pier for harbor and open ocean views.

Laguna Beach
Heisler Park + Main Beach Free 1–1.5 hrs

Heisler Park — clifftop park above the ocean with winding paths, benches, and direct beach access via stairways. The views are exceptional and it's less crowded than Main Beach. Walk south along the cliff edge.

Main Beach Park — volleyball courts, lifeguard tower, wide sandy beach with gentle waves. The most iconic Laguna Beach scene. The town itself has galleries, fish tacos, and a historic arts district (Laguna Beach was a California Plein Air painting colony in the early 1900s).

La Jolla Cove
1100 Coast Blvd, La Jolla, CA 92037 Free · San Diego city park 1–1.5 hrs · Snorkeling, sea lions, tidepools

One of the most beautiful beach coves in Southern California — sandstone cliffs, crystal-clear protected water, and large colonies of sea lions lounging on the rocks year-round. The water is calm enough for snorkeling and diving (70-foot kelp forests and diverse marine life). In July you'll see sea lion pups in the colony with their mothers.

The clifftop path above the cove offers panoramic views of the sea lions and the ocean. At low tide, walk the rocky shore to explore tidal pools. Sunny Jim's Sea Cave — accessible via a tunnel from Cave Store (225 Coast Blvd) — is the only drive-in sea cave in the US open to the public (small entrance fee).

TripAdvisor rates it 4.6 with 13,000+ reviews.

Arrive on the earlier side — parking at La Jolla Cove fills by noon on summer days. Ellen Browning Scripps Park (the clifftop grassy area above the cove) is free and has great sea lion viewing from above.

Evening — Coronado Island
  • Check in at Best Western Plus. ⚠️ Pool and hot tub are closed until December — not available.
  • Hotel del Coronado (1888 Orange Ave — 0.4 mi from hotel) — the iconic red-roofed Victorian grand hotel. Walk the beach side for the famous white sand and the hotel backdrop. The hotel lobby is open to walk through (free). The hotel grounds and beach are public.
  • Coronado Beach — repeatedly ranked one of the best beaches in America. Wide white sand (naturally occurring mica gives it a golden shimmer), gentle waves, calm water. Best at sunset.
  • Dinner options: Little Italy San Diego (10–15 min via Coronado Bridge — the best restaurant neighborhood in San Diego) OR Old Town San Diego (20 min — Mexican food, historic adobe buildings, mariachi, best carne asada tacos in the city).
