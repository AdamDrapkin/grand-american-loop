## 4 Specific Spots at Lake Tahoe

### 📍 Bonsai Rock (East Shore)
- Iconic formation with small trees growing directly out of the rock
- **Activity**: Rent clear kayaks from **@clearlykayak (ClearlyKayak)** and paddle out to it
- Best viewed from the water — kayak rental is the move here

### 📍 Sand Harbor Beach (Nevada State Park, East Shore)
- Crystal clear turquoise water
- Soft sand + massive boulders
- Perfect for swimming or a picnic
- "Doesn't even look real"
- Very popular in summer — arrive early

### 📍 Secret Cove (East Shore, near Incline Village)
- Hidden gem with a short hike down to a secluded beach
- "Quiet, rugged, and insanely blue"
- Less crowded than Sand Harbor

### 📍 Monkey Rock (East Shore)
- Fun hike with "amazing views"
- Creator's favorite sunset spot of the trip
- Shorter trail, accessible for families

---

## Practical Notes
- Clear kayaks from ClearlyKayak for Bonsai Rock — unique and photogenic
- Spring is described as a great time (less crowds, blue water, snow still on peaks)
- All four spots are on the **East Shore** of Lake Tahoe — cluster them in one driving loop
- Sand Harbor requires parking fee + can fill up fast on summer weekends

## Trip Relevance
Lake Tahoe is a confirmed non-negotiable. This post gives us 4 specific, family-friendly activities rather than a vague "go to Tahoe" direction. The east shore cluster allows a focused half-day or full day without covering too much ground. The kayak activity at Bonsai Rock is particularly memorable for kids.
