## California Sections Extracted

### Sequoia National Park (Days 2–3 in their itinerary)
Specific stops named:
- **Congress Trail** — the famous loop through giant sequoias including the General Sherman Tree
- **General Sherman Tree** — largest tree by volume on Earth (~36 feet wide base, 275 feet tall)

### Yosemite National Park (Days 4–5)
Specific stops named:
- **Yosemite Valley** — the iconic valley floor (Half Dome, El Capitan views)
- **Glacier Point Road** — elevated viewpoint looking down into the valley
- **Tunnel View** — the single most photographed viewpoint in Yosemite (valley panorama from the tunnel entrance)
- **Waterfalls** — Yosemite Falls, Bridalveil Fall (both visible from valley floor)

### California Highway 1 Northbound (Day 8)
Specific stops named:
- **Mendocino** — dramatic coastal town on the Mendocino Coast
- **Eureka** — gateway city to Redwood country

### Redwood National & State Parks (Day 9)
Specific stops named:
- **Avenue of the Giants** — iconic 31-mile drive through massive old-growth redwood groves
- **Lady Bird Johnson Grove** — peaceful, accessible old-growth loop trail (~1.4 miles)

## Trip Relevance

### High-Value Specific Stops for Our Plan:
1. **General Sherman Tree + Congress Trail** (Sequoia) — must-do for families, deeply impressive
2. **Tunnel View** (Yosemite) — the single best "arrival" moment in Yosemite, captures the whole valley
3. **Glacier Point Road** (Yosemite) — elevated perspective, especially dramatic at sunset
4. **Avenue of the Giants** — if we include the Northern CA Redwood experience, this 31-mile drive is the gold standard

### For Our Redwood Question:
This creator lists **Avenue of the Giants** + **Lady Bird Johnson Grove** as the Redwood stops — both are near Eureka/Humboldt, ~5–6 hours north of SF. Confirms that the "true" Redwood experience is indeed far north. The question of Muir Woods vs. the real thing remains open.
