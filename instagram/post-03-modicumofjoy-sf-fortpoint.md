## What This Post Is About
**Fort Point National Historic Site** — an underrated, rarely-crowded San Francisco stop that offers the most cinematic view of the Golden Gate Bridge in the city. Not from across the bay — from directly underneath it.

---

## Location Details
**Fort Point National Historic Site**
📍 201 Marine Dr, San Francisco, CA 94129
- Free parking lot available nearby
- Located at the base of the south tower of the Golden Gate Bridge

---

## Why It's Special
- Historic military post that guarded the Golden Gate in the 1800s
- WWII embarkation hub sending troops to the Pacific
- Reborn as arts center after the Army left in the 1970s
- Most tourists who visit the GGB never go here — feels completely removed from the tourist crowd

---

## How to Get the Shot/Experience (from caption)
1. Head straight to the **3rd floor**
2. Walk toward the Golden Gate Bridge side
3. Most windows are sealed, but a **handful of historic windows have NO glass** — fully open to bay air
4. The breeze + bridge view hits instantly

**BONUS TIP**: Head to the **roof** to see the Golden Gate Bridge from **underneath** — "One of the coolest perspectives, super cinematic and rarely crowded"

---

## Trip Relevance
**San Francisco Day 1 or Day 2 activity.** This is exactly the kind of experience the family wants — not feeling like a tourist, discovering something most visitors miss. Fort Point gives the Golden Gate Bridge experience without the selfie-stick crowds of the main viewpoints. 

Pairs perfectly with:
- Crissy Field walk (nearby)
- Baker Beach (5 min drive)
- Heading to Muir Woods afterward (same direction north across the bridge)
